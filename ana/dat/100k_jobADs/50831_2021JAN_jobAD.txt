 Summary: 
 Reporting to the Clinical Supervisor, you will be providing short term telephone, video, and online asynchronous text-based short term counselling for Morneau Shepell's Employee Assistance Program (EAP). In your online group counselling role, you will be leading and facilitating online group counselling (OGC). OGC provides a structured, 4-session group counselling model that focuses on specific clinical issues and goals (e.g. anxiety). 
 Responsibilities: 
 
 short term telephone and video counselling 
 Provide short-term counselling to a diverse set of clients dealing with various personal issues that may or may not be work-related (E-Counselling); 
 Facilitate structured online group counselling chat sessions, focusing on specific clinical issues, using Morneau Shepell-designed resources (OGC). 
 Evaluate client needs and work with them to set objectives and develop a strategy to achieve them, within the terms and conditions of the client’s EAP; 
 Manage the administrative requirements as dictated by Morneau Shepell’s clinical models. 
 
 Succeeding as an E-Counsellor will require the following core qualifications and skills: 
 
 Master’s Degree in Counselling, Psychology, or Social Work; 
 Professional College membership or registration with a mental health association; 
 Valid professional liability insurance covering for counselling with clients located anywhere in Canada; 
 Minimum five (5) years post-graduate counselling experience with multiple populations; 
 Bilingualism in English and French is mandatory; 
 Ability to set realistic objectives and accomplished in solution-focused, narrative, and cognitive-behavioural approaches; 
 Exceptional written communication skills with excellent time-management and organizational skills; 
 Knowledge and comfort using online technologies and fast paced typing (for OGC) are required; 
 Facility with text-based rapport building; experience and/or comfort building online therapeutic relationship and ability to effectively assess clients online is required; 
 Ability to clinically manage high-risk cases, including clients who are a threat to self or others; 
 Good knowledge of community resources; 
 EAP industry experience and digital counselling experience is an asset.
