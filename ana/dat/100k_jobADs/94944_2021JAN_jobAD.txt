 WIAS is one of the leading European research institutes in applied mathematics and is internationally recognized for combining fundamental research in applied analysis and stochastics with complex real-world applications. Situated in the center of Berlin, it offers a highly dynamic research environment. Researchers at WIAS enjoy unique opportunities for mathematical and multidisciplinary collaborations within Berlin and worldwide.

 Diese Seite auf Deutsch 
 The Weierstrass Institute for Applied Analysis and Stochastics (WIAS) is an institute of the Forschungsverbund Berlin e.V. (FVB). The FVB comprises eight non-university research institutes in Berlin which are funded by the federal and state governments. The research institutes are members of the Leibniz Association. 
 WIAS invites applications for an 
 Employee in computer engineering (f/m/d) 
 (Ref. 20/25) 
 starting at  the earliest possible date . 
 Tasks: 
 
 Maintenance and development of WIAS' scientific data and information  management system (including publication management and web server) 
 Support of macOS computers and respective applications 
 IT infrastructure acquisition and sourcing 
 Assistance in user support 
 
 Requirements: 
 
 B.Sc. (or equivalent) degree in computer sciences or related fields (university or university of applied sciences degree) or professional/vocational training in computer sciences or related fields 
 Very good programming skills (Java proficiency, additional languages desirable) 
 Know-how in areas of data management systems and storage of scientific data desirable 
 Knowledge of directory services desirable 
 Very good command of German, good command of English 
 User-oriented communication skills 
 
 We offer: 
 
 A range of tasks offering multiple creative opportunities in an international and science-driven area 
 State-of-the-art servers and challenging tasks in a heterogenous environment 
 A long-term employment including the option of a life-time position 
 A family-friendly work environment (WIAS is certified employer having received the  berufundfamilie  ( workandfamily)  certification) 
 Possibility of further education 
 Salary according to the German TVoeD scale 
 Central location in Berlin 
 
 Please direct your queries to Henrik Pletat, Head of IT (henrik.pletat@wias-berlin.de). The initial appointment is limited to two years and is envisioned to be extended. The work schedule is 39 hours per week. 
 There is equal opportunity for all sexes. When equally suitable in their qualification, severely disabled applicants will receive preferential consideration. We explicitly welcome job applications from people with migration background. 
 Please upload complete application documents including curriculum vitae, photocopies of certificates including a cover letter via our our  online job-application facility  using the button " Apply online ". 
 The search for a qualified candidate will start immediately and continue until the position is filled. 
 We are looking forward to your application!
