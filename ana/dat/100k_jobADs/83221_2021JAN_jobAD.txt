 Evaluate, deploy, monitor, and maintain the latest generation of proof-of-stake blockchain networks 
 Role overview 
 Overview 
 We are looking for a network engineer interested in distributed ledger technology to build, improve, and scale our physical and cloud-based proof-of-stake validation infrastructure. As a blockchain infrastructure engineer, you will work with us in evaluating, deploying, monitoring, maintaining, and upgrading consensus & validation nodes on the most novel and experimental blockchain networks. 
 This role offers the chance to work closely with a team on compelling cross-disciplinary problems in computer science, cryptography, and economics, and enjoy a high degree of independence in working conditions and prioritization. 
 Responsibilities 
 
 Keep track, evaluate, and provide insights into and analysis of novel proof-of-stake distributed ledger protocols 
 Build, improve, maintain, and upgrade our validator software and hardware infrastructure 
 Deploy blockchain consensus nodes in testnets and mainnets, monitor proactively for performance bottlenecks and unusual behavior, optimize performance in production 
 Monitor network traffic and configure physical & virtual minimal-permission access control for fault isolation 
 Automate and scale infrastructure configuration as networks grow 
 
 Qualifications 
 
 Experience with administrating Linux systems and with configuration management (Ansible, Terraform, Docker) 
 Experience with cloud platform services and tooling (GCP, AWS) 
 Experience with metrics collection and monitoring 
 Working understanding of TCP/IP network stack 
 Online availability for unforeseen remotely-fixable issues 
 Travel availability when necessary for in-person fixes 
 Self-motivated & self-organized 
 
 Bonus Qualifications 
 
 Prior experience and interest in blockchain technology 
 Experience in building and scaling distributed, highly available systems 
 Experience in deploying and scaling high-performance database software 
 Interest in and experience with Hardware Security Modules (HSMs)

 Metastate is a research and engineering team focused on implementing cutting-edge research from numerous disciplines (distributed systems, PLT, zero-knowledge cryptography) and deploying them to solve hard problems in the blockchain space, such as scalability, secure smart contract languages, secure randomness or privacy. Metastate is a remote-first team, currently composed of 15 cross-disciplinary researchers and engineers located around the world. Our work culture is characterized by open-allocation, where team members have a high degree of freedom and autonomy in choosing when to work, what to work on, and whom to work with. Metastate was founded in early 2019 by @adrian_brink, @awasunyin and @cwgoes, with extensive experience in the blockchain space and who previously co-founded Cryptium Labs, a security-oriented proof-of-stake validator from the Swiss Alps.
