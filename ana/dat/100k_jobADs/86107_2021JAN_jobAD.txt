 Bereavement Counsellor 
 CMHA Middlesex 
 __London, ON 
 Apply Now 
 Job Summary: 
 The Bereavement Counsellor performs duties and responsibilities that promote recovery and are consistent with the psychosocial rehabilitation model. The Bereavement Counsellor works collaboratively with family members, care-givers, participants, peers and the community to enhance the recovery of participants that had a loss as a result of suicide. 
 This is a part-time, one year contract position, comprising 42 hours per bi-weekly period (0.6 FTE). The successful candidate will be eligible for enrollment in the Healthcare of Ontario Pension Plan (HOOPP). 
 Successful candidates must have a Police Vulnerable Sector Check completed in the last six months. In order to avoid delays in beginning a position, interested applicants who reside in the City of London and are in need of a Police Vulnerable Sector Check (PVSC) are encouraged to apply for one as soon as possible as PVSCs in the City of London are currently taking approximately 2 weeks to be delivered and offers of employment cannot be made without receipt of a PVSC. 
Job Description and Primary Responsibilities: 
Assessment 
 
 Provide consultation services to mental health providers within the organization 
 Encourage referrals to Bereavement Services when appropriate 
 Receive and respond to contacts by community members who have been affected by a loss as a result of suicide, or contacts by those who support participants (e.g. family, friends) 
 Complete thorough assessment of the identified family members/caregiver to determine their need for Bereavement Services 
 Assess the families’ current understanding of mental health issues 
 Determine the status the current familial/caregiver relationship 
 Assess social and environmental factors impeding positive familial/caregiver relationship 
 Assess current level of community supports and resources available to the family 
 Ensure assessments incorporate cultural and ethnic factors 
 
 Direct Service 
 
 Provide individual supportive counselling and skills teaching sessions to identified family members/caregivers in need of one-to-one support 
 Provide individual and group educational opportunities to assist family members/caregivers in learning about loss and grieving and effective coping strategies 
 Participate in youth suicide postvention activities as appropriate 
 Provide family members/caregivers with needed community resources and link and refer to the appropriate community supports 
 Encourage and facilitate growth and recovery by supporting family members/caregivers in developing positive relationships and support strategies 
 Build on family members/caregivers personal strengths, abilities and resources 
 Help family members/caregivers work towards their vision of a positive personal future 
 Provide needed information and education about coping skills, services, resources, self- help and consumer initiatives/supports 
 Encourage and facilitate personal growth and development towards rehabilitation and recovery 
 Motivate the family members/caregiver to learn new skills and behaviours 
 Build on family members/caregiver personal strengths and adjust interventions based on participant response 
 
 Crisis Management 
 
 Have knowledge of range of crisis prevention and intervention approaches including: crisis prevention strategies, crisis planning, crisis intervention, risk assessment and relapse prevention strategies 
 
 Service Coordination and Advocacy 
 
 Assist family members/caregivers to obtain needed resources by educating and supporting them to access resources, linking them to appropriate services 
 Represent CMHA Middlesex on committees within the community as determined to be relevant & appropriate 
 Create & distribute effective advertising materials intended to promote educational events being offered within your program 
 Promote/publicize information regarding the Bereavement Support Program to ensure that bereaved individuals become aware of these services in a timely fashion 
 Upon request, be available to address media inquiries in a manner that is articulate, concise & reflective of organizational values 
 Participate in public education and advocacy

 Current Registration from the Ontario College of Nurses OR Ontario College of Social Workers and Social Service Workers OR Ontario College of Occupational Therapists OR College of Registered Psychotherapists of Ontario 
 Knowledge of loss and grief and its impact on individuals 
 Experience working with individuals who have had a loss as a result of suicide 
 Excellent knowledge of community resources in London-Middlesex 
 Basic computer skills 
 Valid Driver’s License and vehicle to carry out job duties 
 Ability to attend work regularly
