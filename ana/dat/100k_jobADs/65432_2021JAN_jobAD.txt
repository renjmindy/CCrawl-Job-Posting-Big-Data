 ABOUT JOSEPH BRANT HOSPITAL: 
Joseph Brant Hospital (JBH) is a full-service community teaching hospital located in the growing and thriving community of Burlington, Ontario, serving more than 185,000 residents in Halton, Hamilton, Waterdown, Flamborough, Milton and Stoney Creek. JBH is honoured to be recognized as one of Hamilton Niagara's Top Employers for five (5) consecutive years, with a skilled staff of 194 physicians, 1,911 full- and part-time staff and more than 700 volunteers. 
 JBH is a Clinical Education site in conjunction with McMaster University, and designated as an Academic Community Teaching Hospital. Its expanded campus includes the state-of-the-art Michael Lee-Chin & Family Patient Tower, featuring a new Emergency Department, 172 acute inpatient beds, 9 new Operating Rooms and post-anaesthetic care unit to support expanded medical, surgical and outpatient services. JBH is also a partner member of the Burlington Ontario Health Team (OHT). 
 JBH inspires and empowers a culture of caring and this is demonstrated in many ways including our commitment to the health, safety and wellbeing of our people. 
 POSITION SUMMARY: 
We are seeking Clerks to join our Pandemic Response Pool to support Joseph Brant Hospital and the Burlington Community as the Province continues its fight against the global pandemic, providing compassionate care and exemplary service every time. JBH inspires and empowers a culture of caring and this is demonstrated in many ways including our committed to the health, safety and wellbeing of our people. 
 RESPONSIBILITIES: 
 
 Reception and clerical activities of the unit. 
 Disseminate and co-ordinate general information. 
 Facilitate the orderly flow of patients and visitors into and out of the unit. 
 Knowledge of all emergency codes and appropriate action. 
 Maintain inventory of supplies as required. 
 Handle incoming/outgoing mail; photocopying, faxing, courier general typing as required. 
 Transcribe physician's orders, fax med orders to pharmacy and enter all other orders. 
 
 QUALIFICATIONS: 
 
 Medical Office Administration/Unit Clerk Diploma Program or equivalent required.

 Ability to accurately transcribe orders. 
 Excellent telephone manner.

 Excellent interpersonal skills; maintain confidentiality. 
 Excellent command of the English language.

 Demonstrates critical- thinking and decision-making skills. 
 Demonstrated effective documentation skills. 
 Demonstrated excellent communication skills. 
 Excellent command of the English language (written and verbal). 
 Strong organizational skills, with demonstrated ability to prioritize in a fast paced environment.
