 ACCCE welcomes Trump's pick for top job at EPA 
 __Save to read list Published by  Jonathan Rowland , Editor 
 World Coal , Wednesday, 14 December 2016 12:49 
 
 Advertisement 
 The American Coalition for Clean Coal Electricity (ACCCE) has welcomed Donald Trump's nomination of Scott Pruitt to lead the UE Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). 
 "We are pleased with President-elect Donald Trump's nomination of Oklahoma Attorney General Scott Pruitt for EPA Administrator," said Paul Bailey, the ACCCE's Senior Vice President for Policy in a statement. 
 "Pruitt will be a strong advocated for sensible policies that are good to our environment, as well as mindful of the need for affordable and reliable electricity," added Bailey. 
 As Oklahoma Attorney General, Pruitt has been a vocal critic of the EPA and ally of the energy sector in the oil and gas intensive state.

  EIA: US fossil fuel production expected to increase through 2022  
 Friday 15 January 2021 14:45 
 The Energy Information Administration expects production of all fossil fuels – crude oil, coal, dry natural gas, and natural gas plant liquids – to increase in 2022, but forecast fossil fuel production will remain lower than the 2019 peak.
