 Practice Educator 
 An outstanding job opportunity has arisen for a committed Practice Educator to work in an exceptional hospital clinic based in the Hertfordshire area. 
 You will be working for one of UK's leading health care providers 
 This special clinic offers CAMHS Tier 4 low secure services for young people with a wide range of disorders and complex needs 
 As the Practice Educator your key responsibilities include: 
 
 
     Maintaining an identifiable clinical presence and undertake relevant clinical activities to maintain own clinical competency and support staff in developing clinical practice
 
 
 
     Contributing to projects and initiatives as appropriate across CAMHS services
 
 
 
     Keeping the management team abreast of professional, operational or clinical concerns and promote professional practice
 
 
 
     Establish effective communication and good working relationships with multi professional and multi-agency colleagues to support effective service delivery
 
 
 
     Undertaking clinical risk management and clinical audit
 
 
 
     Train and support others in the assessment of patient needs, implementation and the evaluation of programmes of care which are evidence-based
 
 
 
     To develop clinical skills of all newly appointed nursing/HCA's staff to the CAMHS service
 
 
 
 An ideal Practice Educator must have these skills and experience as they are beneficial for this position: 
 
 
     Significant experience of clinical training and teaching in a variety of settings.
 
 
 
     Good personal management skills - time management, prioritising, planning
 
 
 
     Good working knowledge of Safeguarding and Child Protection and recent research issue
 
 
 
     Able to show a can-do attitude always
 
 
 
 **The successful Practice Educator will receive an excellent salary for £31,000 - £37,000 per annum DOE. 
 This exciting position is a Full Time role and in return for your hard work and commitment you will receive the following generous benefits:** 
 
 
     The equivalent of 33 days annual leave - plus your birthday off!
 
 
 
     Free meals and parking
 
 
 
     Wellbeing support and activities
 
 
 
     Career development and training
 
 
 
     Pension contribution
 
 
 
     Life Assurance
 
 
 
     Enhanced Maternity Package
 
 
 
 To be considered for this position you must be qualified as a Nurse either RGN, RMN or RNLD with a valid NMC Pin & have a recognised teaching degree
