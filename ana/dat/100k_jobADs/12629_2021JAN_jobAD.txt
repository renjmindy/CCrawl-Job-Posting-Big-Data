 We Are: 
 The Medical Physics Computer Service team is seeking a System Manager with a strong background in technology project management and team management to join us in New York, NY. 
 We are building and managing a large spectrum of computerized equipment and application development to improve the standard of care in Radiation Oncology and Medical Physics. You will be managing a talent team and working closely with our physicians, clinical researchers, medical physicists, computer scientists, a variety of software engineers, and dedicated IT support specialists in a highly visible role in our organization. 
 You will: 
 
 Report to the Chief of Computer Service, advise and oversees the administration of radiation therapy 
 Provide the business continuity plan of the system during upgrading the disaster recovery 
 Manage, build and train a team to have strong passion and enthusiasm to work on the daily challenges 
 Develop and maintain a detailed Project Plan for each project, defining key milestones, timelines, stakeholders, ensuring that the project stays on time and on budget 
 Coordinate project meetings, take and publish detailed minutes. Engage vendor resources as needed. 
 Work directly to plan and implement software upgrades, including project plans, testing, training, coordination of required resources. 
 Coordinate between department, MSK IT, MSK InfoSec, Vendor to construct risk assessment, security plan for new installation and upgrade. 
 Involve with application development using Agile framework and help manage to deliver the applications on time 
 Help automate the tasks via scripting and programming to improve the efficiency of workflow and promote the DevOps culture in the team 
 
 You need: 
 
 5+ years' experience in team management 
 Experiences in healthcare project management at radiation therapy, radiation oncology, and radiology. 
 Bachelor degree in computer science/Engineering/Management/Physics or related field or equivalent experience 
 Specialist in Windows and Linux scripting languages (PowerShell, Bash, Python preferred). 
 Specialist in network, information security, DICOM 
 Specialist in team culture builds and management 
 Skills in programming using Java, or .NET 
 Experiences working in Agile development environment. 
 Good verbal, writing, and interpersonal skills. 
 
 Nice to have: 
 
 Hands-on experiences in software development 
 Hands-on experiences on DevOps and cloud-based solutions 
 Knowledge in fundamental algorithms in machine learning and deep learning 
 Understanding medical imaging tech stacks (DICOM, PACS, VNA) 
 Understanding Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) 
 Knowledge in healthcare data models

 We believe in communication, openness, and thinking beyond your 8-hour day @ MSK. Its important to us that you have a sense of impact, community, and work/life balance to be and feel your best.

 About Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center 
 As one of the world's premier cancer centers, Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center is committed to exceptional patient care, leading-edge research, and superb educational programs. The close collaboration between our physicians and scientists is one of our unique strengths, enabling us to provide patients with the best care available today as we work to discover more effective strategies to prevent, control, and ultimately cure cancer in the future. Our education programs train future physicians and scientists, and the knowledge and experience they gain at Memorial Sloan-Kettering has an impact on cancer treatment and the biomedical research agenda around the world.
