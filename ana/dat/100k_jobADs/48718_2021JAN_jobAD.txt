 Best Resource to Find Jobs in Canada as a Foreign Resident 
 
 __September 8, 2016 
 __ Canada 
 __ 0 Comments 
 
 If you have just moved to Canada on your IEC visa you will want to look for a Job! 
 You might ask , What will my industry and skill pay in Canada compared to my home country? Use this tools to check !  http://monsterca.salary.com/CanadaSalaryWizard/LayoutScripts/Swzl_NewSearch.aspx  , you might check job boards before and also speak to recruiters , you will want to get a range to target. 
 There is talk   or needed a separate Canadian CV , apart from listing your Canadian Mobile phone , I did not find this was necessary 
 Here are some resources that are useful : 
 Job Boards 
 A few of these you can set email alerts for new jobs and a few you will need to check back on 
 
 www.kijiji.ca  ( Create an Email Alert in your industry / location ) 
 indeed.ca 
 https://career4.successfactors.com/career?company=insight 
 LinkedIn 
 Monster 
 Workable 
 http://www.bctechnology.com/  ( Create an Email Alert in your industry / location ) 
 https://www.welcomebc.ca/Work-or-Study-in-B-C/Work-in-B-C/Find-a-Job-in-B-C  ( Setup a job alert to your email ) 
 Craigslist ( Create an Email Alert in your industry / location ) 
 
 Templates 
 For Jobs on Kijiji and Craiglist and there will be  alot  of them , you should have an email template to send them to speed up this! 
 
 Hello, 
 Just replying to your post on craigslist. I've been in   for 10 years now, and have just started a life in   from  . 
 I've looked through your Job Post and it looks like your responsibilities suit my experience well. During my 10 year career more than half of the time has been working for an   and supporting various sized companies with  . I am a self starter who is also keen to learn   as well as being a team player passing on knowledge where I can. I have attached my C.V. to show my previous work history as well as current qualifications. 
 Any questions let me know and I look forward to hearing from you 
 Thanks 
 
 
 How about Googling your industry and city and reaching out to some of the companies locally? Sometimes this works and shows intuition

 I got your website from a google search for   in   and was wondering if you have any future or current vacancies at your company. 
 I have a strong background with   in the   and looking to continue on my career with an   as yourself, when I move to   in   to utilize my Canada working visa. 
 I have attached my CV for reference

 Recruiters 
 Find any job postings around your industry on the job boards above and get the personal contact details of your recruiter and email them with your situation and C.V. or Linked In. They will actively find work for you from companies not on job boards , usually bigger clients and contracts 
 
 Randstad 
 Robert Half Technologies 
 atc-canada.com 
 itmindfinders.com 
 teemagroup.com 
 teksystems.com
