 Job Description 
 What You’ll Be Doing 
 CIBC’s Fusion Centre leverages best-in-class expertise across the key security domains of Corporate Security, Information Security and Fraud to holistically prevent, detect and respond to multifaceted financial crime threats in order to protect our clients and our bank. 
 The Consultant Fusion Intelligence position is responsible for performing intelligence activities in support of the Fusion Centre mandate. These activities primarily include the collection, aggregation and analysis of a wide variety of internal and external information sources and the production of high quality intelligence outputs. The Consultant Fusion Intelligence provides a complete picture of security-related threats to support decision making, enhance situational awareness and operational readiness and provide unity of effort to effectively mitigate threats. 
 There is 2 openings for the Consultant, Fusion Intelligence role. 
 How You’ll Succeed 
 Stakeholder Management:  Collaborate with key stakeholders across security pillars as part of a coordinated intelligence cycle, applying intelligence best practices and appropriate collection and analysis techniques. Collaboratively and / or independently conduct research to gather, validate and organize threat-related information. 
 Investigations:  Complete in-depth analysis that captures key analytical insights which identify and fill intelligence requirements and gaps; document findings in concise Fusion Intelligence products meeting intelligence-consumer needs. Support complex investigations as an intelligence practitioner and subject matter expert in banking procedures, products and controls. 
 Risk Management:  Assess the quality, relevance and validity of information gathered in relation to threats facing CIBC. Identify potential issues that pose risk to CIBC; take action and contribute to problem and incident management to resolve as quickly and efficiently as possible. 
 This position will require flexibility in managing priorities to meet the demands and the overall objectives of the Fusion Centre. You will be required to effectively manage complex issues, as well as sometimes-fluid deadlines and priorities. This position will operate within a normal office environment; however, it may on occasion require working outside normal office days/hours. 
 Who You Are 
 You can demonstrate experience in 2-5 years financial industry experience in fields such as intelligence, corporate security, information security / cyber, fraud and or / working in a Fusion Centre. 
 You can demonstrate Robust knowledge of intelligence collection and analysis practices with very strong writing skills. Awareness of common threat vectors in the financial sector and ability to discern multi-dimensional security trends and determine impact to the Bank. A strong knowledge of the CIBC Group of Companies and internal banking procedures will be an asset.
