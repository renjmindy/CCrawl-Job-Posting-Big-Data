 Collections Associate FT Temp-To-Hire 
   
   Workway, Inc.   
 __Website    __@workway 
 Collections professionals, are you looking for an opportunity to work with some of the nation’s leading companies? Let Workway open the door for you! We are a professional staffing firm, working with organizations across the country to place exceptional candidates. We have great assignments for Collections Associates with a variety of national client companies. You will negotiate and set up payment plans with individuals who have outstanding bills. 
 Let one of our recruiters assist you in finding and preparing for interviews with our clients. Whether you are interested in a direct hire, temp-to-hire, or temporary position, Workway can help you to put your best foot forward. Contact us today! 
 Job Responsibilities 
 As a Collections Associate, you will manage a queue of delinquent accounts and make contact with borrowers via telephone in order to negotiate payment arrangements. This may involve skip tracing in order to establishing contact with borrowers, then either working out a payment plan or escalating the account to the appropriate department.  Your specific duties as a Collections Associate will include: 
 
 Handling outbound and inbound calls to gather or clarify information 
 Accurately documenting all information pertaining to accounts 
 Meeting or exceeding all performance goals 
 Preparing and/or processing documents (reviewing them for accuracy and completeness; updating information and/or evaluating against policy; comparing elements for consistency or logical relationship, etc.) 
 Performing investigative activities, including skip tracing, to evaluate and determine the whereabouts of customers 
 Receiving researching and responding to incoming questions; providing information, explaining policies and procedures, and/or facilitating a resolution 
 Reviewing, processing and verifying accounts and account balances 
 Performing other duties as assigned 
 
 Job Requirements 
 As a Collections Associate, you must be self-motivated, trustworthy and able to quickly adapt to change in a fast-paced environment. You must also be highly organized and detail-oriented with great follow-through, multitasking and problem-solving skills. It is also important that you have excellent verbal and written communication and interpersonal skills as well as the ability to negotiate mutually beneficial arrangements with a customer.  Specific qualifications for the Collections Associate position include: 
 
 High school diploma or GED; college degree a plus 
 1-5 years of collections experience including business to business collections and accounts receivable 
 Experience managing repossession vendors 
 Ability to utilize 411, reverse lookup, Accurint and other skip trace tools 
 Knowledge of account reconciliation and bankruptcy 
 Working knowledge of the FDCPA 
 Basic computer skills, particularly Outlook, Oracle, Word and Excel 
 Bilingual skills preferred 
 Automobile collections experience a plus 
 CBS call model knowledge a plus
