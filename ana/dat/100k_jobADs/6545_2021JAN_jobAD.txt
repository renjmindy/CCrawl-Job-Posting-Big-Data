 SS Field Tech Level B 
 Penn Power Group - Auburn, WA 
 Posted:  12/1/2020  - Expires:  3/1/2021 
 Job ID: 222412500 
 
 Apply Now

 Job Description 
 Description 
 Performance is reviewed regularly to assess both knowledge and understanding of duties so that further training can be established.  Testing guidelines are available for reference; however, original problem solving and troubleshooting skills are required. 
 Provides support for field operations and fills the role of a lead employee on some projects.  Major duties include physical tasks relating to the testing of electrical equipment and power systems, and effective written and verbal communication.  Perform other assignments as required.  Position requires moderate supervision. 
 Requirements 
 Duties and Responsibilities: 
 Assist in keeping the shop in a neat and orderly condition. 
Assist other field personnel on field projects. 
Provide or assist in on-the-job training of newer technicians. 
Perform tests and inspections on the following equipment (in addition to lower level job descriptions): 
 Medium and High Voltage Cables (including a thorough understanding of IEEE-400 series) 
 Simple Relay Systems 
 Three-Winding Transformers and High Voltage Power Transformers 
 Power metering 
 Automatic Transfer Switches 
 Write reports promptly at end of project using appropriate forms. 
Turn in all required paperwork in a timely fashion (time cards, etc.) 
Follow all Company safety practices. 
Respond promptly to emergency call-out policy. 
 Knowledge, Skills and Abilities: 
 Graduate of applicable Electrical Technical School or Military equivalent and two year minimum same or similar work experience - OR - High school education or equivalent and three years minimum same or similar work experience. 
Possess knowledge equivalent to a NETA Level II technician. 
Basic computer skills including editing, formatting and templates using Microsoft Office, use of internet to research standards, products, and manufacturers. 
Willing to work flexible hours, weekends, holidays and night work.  Must be available for out-of-town and international travel of up to several weeks. 
Regularly required to stand, walk, use hands and fingers, handle or feel objects, tools or controls; reach with hands and arms; stoop, kneel, crouch, or crawl; talk, hear, and smell.  Frequently required to sit, climb and balance.  Specific vision abilities required by this job include close vision, distance vision, basic color differentiation and the ability to adjust focus.  Regularly lift and/or move up to 25 pounds, and frequently lift and/or move 100 pound test sets. 
Valid Driver's License
