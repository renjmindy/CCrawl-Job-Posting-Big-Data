 Peripatetic Nurse 
 A fantastic new job opportunity has arisen for a dedicated Peripatetic Nurse to work in an exceptional nursing home based in the Crook, County Durham area. 
 As a Nurse you will be working for one of UK's leading health care providers 
 This nursing home is a purpose-built residential care home specialising in Alzheimer's, learning disability and dementia-related care 
 As a Nurse your key duties include: 
 
 
     To liaise with all members of the team and key stakeholders including family, friends, visiting professionals and commissioners to ensure holistic care of our residents
 
 
 
     To assess, develop plans and implement plans of care for effective nursing care
 
 
 
     To support senior nursing staff in providing direction, supervision and leadership of team members of the lodge/wing to include direct line management of senior carers
 
 
 
     Participate in the assessment planning, implementation and evaluation of programmes of care in consultation with residents and family
 
 
 
     To demonstrate clinical decision-making skills, displaying evidence-based practice Make effective clinical judgement affecting safe and timely delivery of resident's care
 
 
 
     Be competent in the safe use of clinical equipment to deliver effective residents care
 
 
 
     Maintain accurate written records at all times
 
 
 
     Engage and interact with carers/ family, encourage them to be actively involved in the caring process
 
 
 
     Supervise and instruct senior carers and HCA's to ensure they integrate into the team and operate effectively within the boundaries of their role
 
 
 
     To ensure that residents' safety is maintained and that risk management systems are utilised correctly
 
 
 
     Organise and delegate workload to ensure effective delivery of resident's care is carried out by appropriate skilled members of staff
 
 
 
     Assist in the control and use of resources within defined area of responsibilities Develop Link Role within the team
 
 
 
 An ideal Nurse must have these skills and experience as they are beneficial for this position: 
 
 
     Previous nursing and supervisory experience
 
 
 
     Understand care planning processes and have experience of writing care plans
 
 
 
     Good working knowledge of IT systems
 
 
 
     Able to show a can-do attitude always
 
 
 
 
 **The successful Nurse will receive an excellent salary of £17.00 - £17.50 per hour. 
 This exciting position is a Full Time role and in return for your hard work and commitment you will receive the following generous benefits:** 
 
 
     Holiday entitlement
 
 
 
     Uniform provided
 
 
 
     Free car park
 
 
 
     Career progression
 
 
 
     Pension scheme
 
 
 
     Flexible working opportunities
 
 
 
 To be considered for this position you must be qualified as a Nurse either RGN, RMN or RNLD with a valid NMC Pin
