 Coach Profile 
 |  June Cline, CSP, CSAC 
 How will clients benefit with you as their coach? 
 Clients come away with a clear understand of their own thought process by pinpointing specific thoughts that are supporting or sabotaging their success. This curriculum-based process is rich in discussion points, exercises and a scientifically sound, Nobel Peace Prize nominated, proprietary assessment which, measures their thought process. 
 What results have clients reported after working with you? 
 Clients report stronger interpersonal skills enhancing personal and professional relationships. They learn the art and skill of communication by stepping into someone else’s world naturally bringing down walls of resistance. They are thrilled with my casual but firm style of coaching allowing them to engage, be inspired and grow from their experience. "June is a cross between Mother Teresa and Patton. I would recommend her to any colleague and trust her with any family member." 
 What do clients say about working with you? 
 Clients are amazed and surprised at the insights, learning and FUN they have and the understanding and knowledge they gain about themselves: 
 "Not one day goes by that I don't use the information and skills I learned from you and your coaching process. I am much more joyful with my clients, my work and my family." K. Stephens Interior Designer 
 "Everybody on the team needs to go through this process and learn these techniques to communicate more effectively. Your ability to teach, guide and let others self-discover are masterful. What a gift both you and this process have been for me." M. Keeler 
 What are 5 strengths you have as a coach? 
 My ability to: 
 
 
 Hear what isn’t said. 
 
 
 Identify blockers and blind spots 
 
 
 Easy fears and boost confidence 
 
 
 Specifically identify thought processes that block and/or support success 
 
 
 Bring humor and fun in to the Coaching process 
 
 
 What is your coaching philosophy? 
 
 Get to the heart of the matter: 
Explore their sense of humor, experiences, actions, resiliency and trust. 
 
 What types of people do you typically coach? 
 Executives, managers, supervisors, professional speaker, trainers, workshop leaders, family and friends...anybody with a pulse. 
 What is your professional background? 
 
 
 B.S. in Social Science from Westminster College of Salt Lake City, Ut 
 
 
 Former Financial Industry Vice President/Counselor and Coach 
 
 
 Over 1,000 workshops/speeches on Career development 
 
 
 Professional Organizations such as National Speakers Association 
 
 
 Outsourced Workshop leader for Fortune 500 companies
