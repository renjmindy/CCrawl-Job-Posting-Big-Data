 Your Opportunity: 
The Stollery Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) located at the Sturgeon Community Hospital (SCH) is a 6 bed level 2 NICU that opened in October 2019. The NICU supports the Labor and Delivery (Unit 25) and the Post-Partum (unit 27) at the SCH as well as accepts infants from the other NICUs in the Edmonton Zone. The program utilizes a Family Centered Care philosophy within an interdisciplinary team model. The Registered Nurse (RN) will be required to provide team education and support in neonatal assessment/care to staff of the NICU, Post-Partum and Labor and Delivery units at the SCH. Successful pass of a Math Calculation Competency Assessment of 90% is required prior to the interview process for those who have not taken a math calculation assessment at the Stollery NICUs within the last year. The Edmonton Zone NICU Education Orientation Opportunity is January 18- February 2, 2021 the successful candidate may need to attend this opportunity working a 7.75h day rotation with training at the RAH NICU as a multi-sited NICU position. 
 Description: 
As a Registered Nurse (RN), you will provide a wide variety of nursing services to patients, families, communities and populations, while taking necessary steps to ensure their safety and well-being. In your role, you will utilize nursing processes, through critical thinking, problem solving and decision making, as well as teach, counsel and advocate on behalf of patients and their families. You will play a leadership role in supporting an integrated and holistic approach to patient care, health promotion and maintenance. You will provide safe, quality patient and family centered care while reflecting the shared vision and values of AHS.
