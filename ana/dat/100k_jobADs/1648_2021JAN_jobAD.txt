 Detailed Job Description 
The postdoctoral researcher will work under the supervision of Associate Professor Yosef Zlochower in the areas of numerical simulations of gravitational wave sources and high-performance computation. The postdoctoral researcher will develop new high-performance tools for the Einstein Toolkit. 
 Other responsibilities will include the annual publication of one or more papers about the project(s), the running of simulation codes on supercomputers, writing of codes to advances project(s), collaboration with the faculty supervisor, external collaborators, and Ph.D. students, participation in CCRG activities, as well as participation in a weekly research meeting with the supervisor. 
 Required Minimum Qualifications 
PhD. and research in areas of numerical source modeling and its interface with gravitational-wave data analysis, numerical relativity, and simulation of coalescing astrophysical compact objects such as black-hole and/or neutron star binaries and their interactions with gaseous environments, and initial data for relativistic binary systems. Research in computational astrophysics is also preferred.

 Additional Details 
 _Rochester Institute of Technology, the 10th largest private university in the U.S., is among the worlds leading technological universities. Its 18,000 undergraduate and graduate students from all 50 states and more than 100 countries are enrolled in over 200 academic programs, including 7 interdisciplinary Ph.D. programs. A pioneer in experiential education, RIT was the first to offer undergraduate degree programs in biotechnology, information technology, software engineering, and microelectronic engineering. As home to the National Technical Institute for the Deaf, the university offers unparalleled academic opportunities and services for the deaf and hard-of-hearing. Founded in 1829, RIT has 115,000 alumni throughout the United States and around the world. RIT has been named by the Chronicle of Higher Education as one of the Great Colleges to Work For.  About Rochester Institute of Technology 
 Founded in 1829, Rochester Institute of Technology is a diverse and collaborative community of engaged, socially conscious, and intellectually curious minds. Through creativity and innovation, and an intentional blending of technology, the arts and design, we provide exceptional individuals with a wide range of academic opportunities, including a leading research program and an internationally recognized education for deaf and hard-of-hearing students. Beyond our main campus in Rochester, New York, RIT has international campuses in China, Croatia, Dubai, and Kosovo.
