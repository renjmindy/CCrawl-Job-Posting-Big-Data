 Unit Manager - Senior Nurse 
 We are looking for experienced Unit Manager /  Senior Registered Nurses  to join our team to deliver outstanding Specialist Nurse-led care for adults with complex needs. 
 opening Spring 2019 
 We are pleased to announce the opening up  a specialist nurse-led care home for adults with moderate to complex care needs, including but not limited to: Neuro-disability, Brain injury & stroke, Mental ill-health, Profound learning disability, Complex dementia, Autism, Physical disability, Huntington's Disease. 
 As a Unit Manager with this company , you will take the role of a senior nurse on duty and have responsibility for a team of nursing and care staff.  You will lead by example, delivering hands-on nursing care and providing strong leadership to your team.  You will have a person centred ethos and be committed to enriching the lives of the individuals in your care. 
 On a day to day basis, you will have clinical, people and general management responsibilities and these will include: 
 
 
     Conducting pre-admission assessments, assessing client care requirements
 
 
 
     Working with the MDT to ensure that care provided is appropriate and relevant to the individual's care package
 
 
 
     Provide safe and effective processes for the ordering, handling, storage, administration, recording and disposal of drugs
 
 
 
     Maintain cost effective controls of all clinical supplies (including ordering, storage, stock rotation and correct usage)
 
 
 
     Review and document care plans on a regular basis
 
 
 
     Positively promote the home
 
 
 
     Participate in an on-call system to support the nurse in charge
 
 
 
     Participate in recruitment, supervision and training of staff
 
 
 
     Deputising for the Registered Manager as necessary
 
 
 
     Report all compliments, comments and complaints
 
 
 
     Complying with all legal and regulatory policies and procedures
 
 
 
     Promoting choice, independence, dignity and respect
 
 
 
     Adhering to the NMC Code and updating your professional knowledge
 
 
 
     You should hold a level 1 nursing qualification (RGN/RMN/RNLD) and have a current PIN.
