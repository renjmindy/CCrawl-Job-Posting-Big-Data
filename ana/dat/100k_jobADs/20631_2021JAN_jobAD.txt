 ABOUT JOSEPH BRANT HOSPITAL: 
Joseph Brant Hospital (JBH) is a full-service community teaching hospital located in the growing and thriving community of Burlington, Ontario, serving more than 185,000 residents in Halton, Hamilton, Waterdown, Flamborough, Milton and Stoney Creek. JBH is honoured to be recognized as one of Hamilton Niagara's Top Employers for five (5) consecutive years, with a skilled staff of 194 physicians, 1,911 full- and part-time staff and more than 700 volunteers. 
 JBH is a Clinical Education site in conjunction with McMaster University, and designated as an Academic Community Teaching Hospital. Its expanded campus includes the state-of-the-art Michael Lee-Chin & Family Patient Tower, featuring a new Emergency Department, 172 acute inpatient beds, 9 new Operating Rooms and post-anaesthetic care unit to support expanded medical, surgical and outpatient services. JBH is also a partner member of the Burlington Ontario Health Team (OHT). 
 JBH inspires and empowers a culture of caring and this is demonstrated in many ways including our commitment to the health, safety and wellbeing of our people. For more information, visit www.josephbranthospital.ca. 
 POSITION SUMMARY: 
We are seeking Registered Practical Nurses to join our Pandemic Response Pool to support Joseph Brant Hospital and the Burlington Community as the Province continues its fight against the COVID-19 global pandemic, providing compassionate care and exemplary service every time. As a Registered Practical Nurse in the Pandemic Response Pool, you will be responsible for acute patient populations across multiple clinical units, providing both inpatient and outpatient medical/surgical care. 
 QUALIFICATIONS: 
 
 Current Certificate of Competence in the Province of Ontario. 
 Preference will be given to candidates who have current or recent clinical experience in Medicine, Surgery, Emergency, or other acute care. 
 Ability to adapt and respond effectively in rapidly changing situations or environments. 
 Collaborate and work effectively within a multidisciplinary team. 
 Commitment to upholding IPAC standards and strict adherence to IPAC policies. 
 Excellent communication skills. 
 Commitment to quality and patient safety, considers safety of self and co-workers.
