 63 Percent Of All U.S. Jobs Created Since 1990 Have Been Low Wage Jobs

 63 Percent Of All U.S. Jobs Created Since 1990 Have Been Low Wage Jobs 
 December 1, 2019  by  Michael Snyder 
 If you have a good paying job, you should probably try to hold on to it as hard as you can, because those types of jobs are steadily becoming rarer. Since 1990, the U.S. economy has produced millions of jobs, but as you will see below nearly two-thirds of them have been low wage jobs. Of course this is one of the biggest factors causing the systematic erosion of the American middle class. Today, half of all U.S. workers  make less than $33,000 a year , but meanwhile the cost of living has been steadily increasing. Housing costs, health insurance and other basic necessities have been rising much faster than our paychecks have, and this has put an enormous amount of financial stress on hard working American families.
