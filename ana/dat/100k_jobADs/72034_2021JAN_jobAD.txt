 Blood Collection Specialist, Associate (Phlebotomist) - Paid Training 
 Bloodworks - Federal Way, WA 
 Posted:  12/1/2020  - Expires:  3/1/2021 
 Job ID: 222419411 
 
 Apply Now  
 I have already applied

 __Print  __Email  __Share 
 
 Job Description 
 Help save lives every day!  This entry-level position in training to become a Blood Collection Specialist II is responsible for performing whole blood procedures with an uncompromising commitment to quality standards, the safety of the blood supply and the donor experience. Our world-class training program will provide you with the phlebotomy skills and knowledge needed to evaluate potential donors and perform blood collections. This position works directly with our volunteers to provide a welcoming and pleasant environment for our donors. 
 Responsibilities: 
 
 Take vital signs including blood pressure, pulse, temperature, and hemoglobin 
 Screen potential donors/patients and evaluate their eligibility following Bloodworks' policies/procedures while answering donor questions that ensure they are giving informed consent for donation and blood testing 
 Perform vein punctures for whole blood and collect samples for testing 
 Follow universal precautions and bio-hazard safety standards for handling blood. 
 Properly document and perform quality controls to uphold Bloodworks and FDA standards to maintain the safety of the blood supply 
 Interact with team members and donors in a knowledgeable and professional manner 
 
 Position Requirements: 
 
 High School education or GED equivalent required 
 One year of work experience in a role where the primary focus is on customer service 
 Driver's license for 3 years with clean driving record 
 Excellent hand- eye coordination 
 Ability to stand for prolonged periods of time, bend and reach to perform repetitive motions, lift and carry up to 60 pounds 
 Work flexible shifts with the ability to travel to various work locations- schedules may change frequently and unexpectedly. Work may include overnight, holidays and weekend work away from home 
 Ability to attend a 6-week training program 
 Excellent verbal and written communication skills 
 Proficiency with Microsoft Office programs: Word, Excel, Outlook 
 
 Benefits and Perks 
 We offer a competitive benefits package including medical, dental, and vision insurance, company paid for life and disability, 401(k) contribution, education reimbursement and up to 24 days of PTO your first year.
