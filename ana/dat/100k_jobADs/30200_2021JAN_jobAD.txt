 Deputy Manager 
 An outstanding new job opportunity has arisen for a committed Deputy Manager to work in an exceptional nursing home based in the Burscough, Ormskirk area. 
 As the Deputy Manager you will be working for one of UK's leading health care providers 
 The nursing home is a superb refurbished building situated on a corner plot enjoying views over picturesque scenery, as well as providing more than ample car parking space. 
 The service is truly a home for life due to being dual registered home catering for service users with both residential and nursing needs 
 As the Deputy Manager your key responsibilities include: 
 
 
     You will be responsible for assisting the Registered Home Manager in managing and developing high quality services for all the residents and the management and development of the staff team
 
 
 
     To instil a culture of care and continuous improvement across the service, actively working with the team, the residents and their families to develop a centre of excellence for person centred care
 
 
 
     Ensure the promotion of high standards of care
 
 
 
     Fulfil the duties of the Home Manager where required
 
 
 
     Ensuring the home runs in accordance with CQC guidelines and that the team are managed appropriately and that the home is run in a smooth and efficient manner
 
 
 
 An ideal Deputy Manager must have these skills and experience as they are beneficial for this position: 
 
 
     You will have a clear understanding of leadership responsibilities in elderly/social care with excellent communication and interpersonal skills
 
 
 
     Proven management experience within the sector
 
 
 
     Undertake regular audits to assess the quality of care given to the residents and present those findings at managers meetings
 
 
 
     Experience of working in a similar environment, you will be able to demonstrate a track record in leading, motivating, managing and developing staff
 
 
 
 **The successful Deputy Manager will receive an excellent salary of £17.76 per hour and the annual salary for this position is £36,940.50 per annum. 
 This exciting position is a Full Time role working 40 hours a week. 
 In return for your hard work and commitment you will receive the following generous benefits:** 
 
 
     2 Weekly Pay
 
 
 
     28 days annual leave (pro rata)
 
 
 
     Pension Scheme
 
 
 
     Cost of DBS Check paid for you
 
 
 
     Comprehensive paid induction training and ongoing development through our training academy
 
 
 
     Full support of a strong Head Office Team including HR, Finance, Recruitment, Payroll and Administration
 
 
 
 To be considered for this position you must be qualified as a Nurse either RGN or RMN with a valid NMC Pin
