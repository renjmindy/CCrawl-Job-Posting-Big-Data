 Back to Work: From cloud certification programs to virtual job fairs 
 In this The Future of Work Blog: Getting back to work around the country 
 By 
 Laura Aka 
 - 
 November 30, 2020

 The jobless claims for the week ending November 21 totaled 778,000, according to the  Department of Labor . That figure is higher than the 733,000 expected by economists who were surveyed by Dow Jones. For some perspective, in mid-March, weekly claims were roughly 225,000. 
 The unemployment rate for November will be released on Friday by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. 
 WorkingNation is always looking for employment and training opportunities around the country. Here are some recent findings from eight states or territories. 
 From  Arizona : Prescott eNews reports the Arizona Commerce Authority announces an initiative to train and certify 5,000 students for entry-level cloud computing careers by June 2022. 
 From  Colorado : Your Hub reports the Colorado Homebuilding Academy is bringing its construction boot camps to Commerce City. 
 From  Idaho : Post Register reports a $1 million dollar grant will cover the tuition for 30 graduate students at Idaho State University over the next five years. 
 From  Massachusetts : BusinessWest reports Holyoke Community College has developed career-track courses related to occupations in the cannabis industry. 
 From  Missouri : West Plains Daily Quill reports the Missouri Office of Workforce Development is continuing its series of virtual job fairs through the end of the year. 
 From  North Dakota : AJOT reports a new Amazon fulfillment center in Fargo will create 500 jobs next year. 
 From  Puerto Rico : NimB reports Bayer Crop Science Puerto Rico is recruiting talent from the island’s deaf and partially deaf community to fill soy pollinator positions with plans to expand job offerings throughout the season. 
 From the  Virgin Islands : The St. John Source reports a small business owner has established a skills training and entrepreneurship program targeting youth, ages 16 to 21 years old.
