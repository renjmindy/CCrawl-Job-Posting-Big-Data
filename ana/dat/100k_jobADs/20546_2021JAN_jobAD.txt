 Maintenance / NPI Technician 
 MRO Electronic Supply 
 __Calgary, AB 
 Apply Now 
 MRO Electronic Supply is an ISO 9001 certified custom cable assembly manufacturer specializing in over-molded cable assemblies for harsh environments and a distributor of industrial electronic components. We specialize in service to a number of industries, including Oil & Gas, Mining, Agriculture, Automotive, Telecom and Military. We take pride in our history and growth (established in 1991, currently 100+ employees), as well as our commitment to quality and customer service.

 Position Overview 
 This is a blend position involving both the maintenance of tools and equipment around the manufacturing facility and assisting with the introduction of new products into volume production by defining requirements, reviewing designs and driving technical solutions for problems encountered during the proto/pilot phase of the product. 
 Essential Job Functions: 
 
 
 Performing mechanical and electrical troubleshooting for manufacturing equipment 
 
 
 Reviewing existing machinery, equipment, tools and safety methods 
 
 
 Proactively detecting manufacturer and machine imperfections in order to access warranty 
 
 
 Completing regular and preventative maintenance of equipment 
 
 
 Performing emergency repairs on equipment where necessary 
 
 
 Ensuring proper safety protocols are in place to work on complex high voltage and low voltage electrical and mechanical issues 
 
 
 Ensuring safe operation of all equipment and machinery on the warehouse floor 
 
 
 Working with the internal OHS team to ensure the warehouse exceeds all audit standards and complies with all OHS rules including proper reporting 
 
 
 Act as link between Suppliers, Engineering and Manufacturing team when new products are released 
 
 
 Supporting the NPI team during new design reviews by representing manufacturing and suppliers 
 
 
 Diagnostic troubleshooting, rework/repair of non-conforming manufactured products and production equipment 
 
 
 Perform revision updates, rework or repair per approved disposition using a variety of tools and soldering equipment 
 
 
 Prepare and manage documentation of parts and products 
 
 
 Comply with policies, guidelines and regulatory requirements per the Quality System 
 
 
 Maintain a clean and organized work area to facilitate manufacturing functions 
 
 
 Report problems with quality, processes, equipment and materials to Production Supervisor 
 
 
 Assemble and test product and various electro-mechanical production equipment 
 
 
 Qualification Requirements: 
 
 
 Skilled in the use of hand and power tools 
 
 
 Ability to take apart machines, equipment, or devices to remove and replace defective parts 
 
 
 Ability to check blueprints, repair manuals, or parts catalogs as necessary 
 
 
 Ability to use common tools such as hammers, hoists, saws, drills, and wrenches 
 
 
 Experience with precision measuring instruments or electronic testing devices 
 
 
 Experience performing routine maintenance 
 
 
 Strong organizational and follow up skills 
 
 
 Eye for detail 
 
 
 Forklift driving experience and Licence 
 
 
 Ability to maintain focus while working individually 
 
 
 Strong time management skills 
 
 
 Intermediate to advanced computer skills 
 
 
 Electrical/electronics technical knowledge and experience - 1 year 
 
 
 Ability to read and interpret technical drawings, schematics and specs 
 
 
 Good balance of theoretical and practical experience and "hands on" in a manufacturing and production environment 
 
 
 Experience using test equipment 
 
 
 Soldering experience is considered a valuable asset 
 
 
 
 Ability to work independently with limited supervision 
 
 
 Great time management skills 
 
 
 Ability to work under pressure and to meet deadlines 
 
 
 Physical Demands: 
 Provide hands on job function training to coworkers as needed. Ability to use hands to finger, handle, or feel. Ability to frequently sit, stand, walk, reach within hands and arms length, climb or balance, stoop, kneel, and crouch. Ability to regularly lift and/or move up to 10 pounds, frequently lift and/or move up to 25 pounds, and occasionally lift and/or move up to 50 pounds.
