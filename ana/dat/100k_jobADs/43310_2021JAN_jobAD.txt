 Gleeson Recruitment Group are partnering with an exciting business currently recruiting for a Group Finance Manager position within their Birmingham team. The role assists the Financial Controller and their key stakeholders in analysing and reporting on the key business drivers, with emphasis on company and store performance and cost control. 
 Key Responsibilities 
 As Group Finance Manager you will be responsible for the following: 
 
 Ensuring the group is preparing financial reporting in accordance with IFRS accounting standards and group requirements 
 Management of month/quarter/year end financial reporting processes and assisting with the audit process 
 Review and preparation of balance sheet reconciliations and fluctuation analysis 
 Review and analyse reserves across the group 
 The ability to understand explain complex accounting issues to non-finance stakeholders 
 Understanding differences between group and local level accounting requirements and impacts 
 Support local teams with statutory accounting preparation and issues as they arise 
 To identify process and reporting improvement opportunities at consolidation 
 Investigation of and reporting on any identified control weaknesses or control failures 
 Recommend and help implement control improvements necessary to the financial reporting process 
 Support management in building controls awareness and skills among local finance teams with a view to strengthening internal controls 
 The development of corporate accounting procedures and best practices 
 Assist with implementing new processes that provide a more robust control environment across sites 
 
 Skills & Experience 
 The ideal candidate for this Group Finance Manager position will possess the following 
 
 A highly motivated Qualified Accountant (ACA/ CIMA/ ACCA) preferably making their second move from an accountancy practice. 
 Advanced Excel skills with ability to handle large volumes of data. 
 Strong business partnering skills and analytical skills - able to provide calculated, commercial and operational support as well as challenge 
 Attention to detail and "Can do" attitude 
 Ability to communicate with stakeholders across the business via appropriate means 
 Organised approach with the ability to manage multiple tasks simultaneously and meet deadlines 
 Experience in a manufacturing or engineering operation is advantageous 
 
 What's On Offer? 
 
 Salary of £50,000/60,000 + benefits 
 Excellent career progression opportunities 
 Opportunity to work with a market-leading brand 
 A positive and down-to-earth culture 
 Flexible working arrangements 
 Eligibility for a generous bonus scheme and car allowance
