 ABOUT JOSEPH BRANT HOSPITAL: 
Joseph Brant Hospital (JBH) is a full-service community teaching hospital located in the growing and thriving community of Burlington, Ontario, serving more than 185,000 residents in Halton, Hamilton, Waterdown, Flamborough, Milton and Stoney Creek. It is honoured to be recognized as one of Hamilton Niagara's Top Employers for five (5) consecutive years, with a skilled staff of 194 physicians, 1,911 full- and part-time staff and more than 700 volunteers. 
 JBH is a Clinical Education site in conjunction with McMaster University, and designated as an Academic Community Teaching Hospital. Its expanded campus includes the state-of-the-art Michael Lee-Chin & Family Patient Tower, featuring a new Emergency Department, 172 acute inpatient beds, 9 new Operating Rooms and post-anaesthetic care unit to support expanded medical, surgical and outpatient services. JBH is also a partner member of the Burlington Ontario Health Team (OHT). JBH inspires and empowers a culture of caring and this is demonstrated in many ways including our commitment to the health, safety and wellbeing of our people. For more information, visit www.josephbranthospital.ca. 
 Our Employee Health, Safety, Wellness Department is seeking Employee Health Nurses as we continue to support our health care heroes as the Province continues its fight against the global pandemic supported with compassionate care and exemplary service every time.  POSITION SUMMARY: 
Reporting to the Sr. Manager, Labour Relations & Employee Health Services the Occupational Health Nurse (OHN) is a valued team member supporting our commitment to provide a safe and healthy workplace for our people. 
 As a member of the Employee Heath, Safety and Wellness Team you will be supporting the oversight, delivery and services offered to our employees in alignment with our CARE (Compassion. Accountably. Respect. Excellence.) values. 
 RESPONSIBILITIES: 
 
 Provides primary care and assessments for Employee and non-Employee injuries and illness 
 Conduct pre-placement health assessments 
 Provide immunological services including TB testing and reading to support medical surveillance programs; as required 
 Assist in providing case management of short term disability (STD), long term disability (LTD), Workplace Safety and Insurance Board (WSIB) claims 
 In consultation with the team, provide support to Managers on attendance related challenges related to medical conditions 
 Promote on site healthy workplace promotion and education activities 
 Assist, advise and support the abilities team in formulating modified work/accommodation plans 
 In consultation with the team, identify, manage and respond to public health notices in partnership with IPAC, as required 
 Accurately maintains documentation of employee health files to ensure currency and validity of information 
 
 QUALIFICATIONS: 
 
 Current Certificate of Registration, in good standing, with the College of Nurses of Ontario 
 BScN preferred 
 Certification in Occupational Health nursing, COHN(C) or certificate/diploma in OHS nursing. Applicants actively engaged in the certification process with some courses completed will be considered 
 Membership with the OOHNA preferred 
 3 to 5 years of Employee health experience preferred 
 Recent experience with COVID-19 pandemic infection control, illness triage and testing guidelines are an asset required 
 Demonstrated health assessment skills and relevant knowledge to the Public Hospitals Act, and Communicable Disease Surveillance Protocols 
 Knowledge of relevant and current legislation including: OHSA, WSIA, OHRC required 
 Knowledge of health and safety procedures, as well as Employee wellness programs 
 Ability to handle work with sensitive issues and protect confidentiality of medical information 
 Sound judgment in the application of nursing and safety principles 
 Strong communication skills with the ability to establish positive working relationships with internal/external partners 
 Excellent organizational and time management skills are key to support your work in a fast paces health care, multi-union environment 
 Proficient in MS Office required, working knowledge of Parklane an asset 
 Regular attendance is required
