 Aurora Cannabis

 QUALITY ASSURANCE COORDINATOR / AUDITOR 
 Reporting to the Manager, Quality Assurance, this Quality Assurance Coordinator/Auditor will be based out of our Pemberton facility in British Columbia, and responsible for the following: 
 MAIN RESPONSIBILITIES: 
Represent QA as an ambassador for regulatory compliance with respect to Cannabis Regulations, GMP, GPP, and company’s SOPs 
Assist in developing and writing SOPs for all areas of the facility, ensuring compliance with Cannabis Regulations and GPP/GMP 
Assist with conducting investigations regarding deviations or complaints, including gathering of data, and identification of root cause 
Provide guidance to manufacturing with respect to GPP, GDP, and GMP 
Build and maintain good working relationships with manufacturing to promote openness and compliance 
Perform on floor auditing of records including sanitation, logbooks, and PBRs 
Perform initial batch record review for completed PBRs from growing, oil production, and bottling. 
Conduct sanitation and pest control review to ensure compliance. 
Perform verification (Labels, weights, fulfilled orders) 
Perform required line clearances during manufacturing operations 
Verify electronic inventory records of dried cannabis, packaged product, and live plants 
Perform QA duties for materials management such as release label creation, application, and inventory follow up 
Participate in Self Inspection Process 
Assist with tracking metrics around compliance and efficiency and recommend measures to improve productivity and product quality. 
Assist QA Associates, and QA Manager when necessary 
 REQUIREMENTS 
 3-5 years' experience in the Quality Assurance Field 
Degree or diploma in a science-related field 
Experience working in a regulated environment, pharmaceuticals, or natural health product manufacturing environment is preferred 
Understanding of Quality Management Systems including batch record review, batch release, document control, change control, deviations, investigations, and CAPA 
Ability to understand, follow and mentor production staff regarding SOP compliance 
Strong attention to detail and organization skills 
Ability to multitask and adapt to an evolving work environment 
Fluency in MS Office is mandatory 
Competency of technological writing (SOPs, Investigation Summary) 
Ability to work independently, with strong attention to detail 
Ability to both speak and write clearly in English 
 We would like to thank all applicants for their interest, but only those selected for an interview will be contacted.
