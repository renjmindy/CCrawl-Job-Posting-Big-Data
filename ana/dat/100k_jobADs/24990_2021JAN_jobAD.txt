 Quality Improvement Lead – Infection Control 
 An outstanding new job opportunity has arisen for a dedicated Quality Improvement Lead to contribute to a proactive and robust clinical governance and quality assurance framework that enables the healthcare company to measure and continuously improve the compliance and quality of services 
 You will not need to visit all homes as you will be home or office based with the potential to go out to specific sites if needed 
 As the Quality Improvement Lead your key responsibilities include: 
 
 
     To ensure Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) is addressed in all areas of the Trust and reflected throughout all activity related to healthcare provision
 
 
 
     Taking accountability for infection prevention all new business, new build and refurbishment
 
 
 
     Ensuring compliance to the HCAI code (DH 2010) for CQC requirements
 
 
 
     To provide advice on the purchase, introduction and use of equipment in relation to IPC issues
 
 
 
     To support in local waste management but especially in the management of the clinical waste stream
 
 
 
 An ideal Quality Improvement Lead must have these skills and experience as they are beneficial for this position: 
 
 
     Working knowledge of Care Quality Commission Regulations, Mental Capacity Act, Health and Safety, Safeguarding and all other relevant legislation and policies
 
 
 
     Clear understanding of the Care Act and its implications
 
 
 
     Experience of working in healthcare or social care sector
 
 
 
     Have worked in a multisite organisation previously
 
 
 
 **The successful Quality Improvement Lead will receive an excellent salary from £38,000 to £40,000 per annum. 
 This exciting position is a Full Time role working 37.5 hours a week. 
 In return for your hard work and commitment you will receive the following generous benefits:** 
 
 
     Company will pay for the annual renewal of your NMC PIN (if nurse qualified)
 
 
 
     Support with continual professional development
 
 
 
     Specialist internal and external training
 
 
 
     Leadership development opportunities
 
 
 
     Relevant workshops, conferences and networking events
 
 
 
     Company pension scheme
 
 
 
     Sick pay
 
 
 
 To be considered for this position you must have a qualification in Infection Control and Prevention or qualified as a Nurse
