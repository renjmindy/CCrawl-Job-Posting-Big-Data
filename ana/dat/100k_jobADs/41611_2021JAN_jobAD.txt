 Electrician Technician 
 Job description 
 
 
 $42.00 per hour 
 
 
 Rockhampton local role 
 
 
 ASAP Start 
 
 
 About Our Client: 
 Our client provides valued fire services to thousands of local, regional, and national businesses, government agencies and organisations. 
 About the Role: 
 WorkPac are currently seeking an experienced Fire Protection Technician in Rockhampton for an asap start. This position will hold a Monday to Friday roster, 38 hours per week, 3 months in duration with competitive rates available for the successful applicant. 
 This position would suit an experienced Domestic Electrician with strong safety experience and preferably experience in fire related servicing. 
 Duties and Responsibilities: 
 
 
 Install fire detection and alarm systems, fire suppression systems and firefighting equipment. 
 
 
 inspect and service equipment and systems to ensure they are operational. 
 
 
 Skills and Experience: 
 
 
 Electrical Trade Certificate 
 
 
 Extinguishing Agent Handling Licence 
 
 
 Current "C" Class Driver's License and Own Transport 
 
 
 Ability to pass a full Pre-Employment Medical and Drug & Alcohol Screen 
 
 
 Must be either an Australian Citizen or a holder of an Australian permanent residency visa to be eligible to apply for this position 
 
 
 Job Reference Number: BSINDJ2020105690 
 How to Apply: 
 Click on the Apply button or for more information please contact: 
 WorkPac Recruitment Co-ordinator: Alanna Keehn on 0429 349 149 
 About WorkPac 
 WorkPac is Australia's leading, privately owned recruitment provider to the mining, construction, engineering, light industrial and healthcare sectors. We are proud to partner with some of the country's leading companies to deliver rewarding permanent, contract, temporary and traineeship recruitment opportunities. 
 At WorkPac, we recognise we are strengthened by diversity. We are committed to providing a work environment in which everyone is included, treated fairly and with respect. We are an Equal Opportunity employer and we encourage applications from women and Indigenous people.
