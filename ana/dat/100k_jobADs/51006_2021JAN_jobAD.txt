 Emotech is based in London, UK, and found in 2014. We are dedicated to exploring a multimodality system that blends various machine learning algorithms, allowing for bespoke solutions to AI needs for software and hardware. Emotech was selected by TechCrunch as one of the Top 14 European Startups and then Top 30 Business Cases in China by Financial Times. In 2019, Emotech introduced the first multimodality AI English teaching assistant solution launched in Huawei HDC and awarded the first London Business Award. Emotech has been in collaboration with renowned universities since 2015 starting from UCL, Imperial College, the University of Edinburgh to the University of Cambridge, University of Oxford, CMU. 
 Overview 
 We are seeking a passionate and knowledgeable Speech Applied Research Scientist who can help us to improve our cutting edge speech technology. As a Speech Applied Research Scientist, you will be collaborating with scientists and engineers to bring research into production. This includes refactoring, measuring and improving the performance of the existing frameworks and pipelines. Also you will be collaborating with scientists to create and improve various Speech Technology and Machine Learning models and track their performance over time. 
 Responsibilities: 
 
 Work with other Speech/ML researchers to prototype speech and machine learning algorithms. 
 Adapt existing algorithms to maximise their performance for run-time deployments 
 Publish at top-tier speech conferences (ICASSP, Interspeech, ASRU, ...) 
 Design and develop state-of-the-art machine learning models 
 Advise the engineering team with development of tools for profiling, analysing and debugging 
 Advise the data engineers with efficient data collection and labelling tasks so data is useful for training of the new acoustic and language models 
 
 Required Qualifications: 
 
 At least 5 years of experience in the industry 
 Strong grasp of fundamentals: linear algebra, finite state machines, discrete and continuous optimization, supervised and unsupervised methods, generative and discriminative methods. 
 Masters Degree in Computer Science or other relevant areas. PhD preferred. 
 Expertise in one or more focus areas or their subfields: acoustic modelling, language modelling, audio-visual ASR, deep learning, graphical models, search, model compression, multi-task learning, etc, 
 Extensive experience with deep learning frameworks Tensorflow, Caffe, PyTorch, Theano, etc (any of them) 
 Excellent software engineering skills with working experience in Python, C++ or Rust (at least two). 
 Proven track record of experience in relevant areas (significant industry experience and/or strong publication record at top venues like ICASSP, Interspeech, ICML, NeurlIPS, …) 
 Extensive experience and understanding of speech frameworks (not the ability to run the recipes): Kaldi (both user and developer perspectives) 
 Ability to quickly prototype state-of-the-art algorithms following recent literature developments. 
 Ability to write high quality code 
 
 Advantageous: 
 
 Ability to work with large noisy datasets 
 Speech related development experience in industry 
 Proficient knowledge of Tensorflow, PyTorch or similar DL frameworks 
 Strong grasp of data structures, algorithms and communication protocols.
