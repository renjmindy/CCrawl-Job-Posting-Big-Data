 11. Data Entry 
 If you are good with software such as Microsoft Excel, you will surely love data entry jobs. Data entry jobs require little to no training. If you can manipulate and use Microsoft Excel, you can easily succeed here.

 12. Website Testing 
 Website testing involves reviewing different websites and giving opinions on how they can be improved. 
 Your opinion will help improve the usability of websites. You will help improve the navigability, ease-of-use, relevancy, and content of websites. 
 Testing a single website can take you about 15-20 minutes. Most website testing jobs pay between $8 and $20. 
 You can find website testing jobs from sites such as UserTesting, WhatUsersdo, UserTest, Enroll, UTest, UserFeel, Start-Up Lift, Analysia, Amazon Mechanical Turk and Userlytics. 
 13. Become a Youtuber 
 Starting a Youtube channel is a great way to earn passively. The videos you create today can make you money for many years to come. 
 What I love most about YouTubing is that you get to produce content on subjects you like. You will never have a dull day at work. You can vlog about anything you like. 
 Besides, you get to select your work schedule. You can record your videos once per week. You will, however, need to set aside some time to edit your videos. 
 You will also love that you can start with whatever resources you have before growing your channel. You can take videos using your smartphone before you upgrade yourself. 
 Starting may be difficult, but once you've grown your channel and have acquired a considerable following and audience, things will be smooth for you. 
 14. Remote customer service 
 If you are good with people, consider getting a remote customer service job. Your job as a customer service rep will vary depending on your employer. You should, however, expect to interact with customers via phone calls or social media platforms.

 You also need to have a workspace or a home office with no interruptions to work as a remote customer service. 
 15. Captioning 
 To increase the viewership of movies and programs, subtitles are usually added at the bottom of the screen. As a captioner, you'll be generating these subtitles for movies and films. 
 You will need a good set of headphones, a serene environment and attentiveness to succeed in captioning. You have to type out what the characters are saying accurately. 
 You can source for captioning jobs from companies such as Caption Max, Crowdsurf, ASC Services, and Caption Media Group. 
 What I love most about captioning is that it requires minimal initial investment. You can make around $20-$50 for captioning an hour-long video.
