 Job Description 
 Providence Health 
 Founded as Providence Hospitals in 1938 by the Sisters of Charity of St. Augustine, Providence's two free standing full-service hospitals and numerous satellite practices maintain a true devotion to advanced clinical expertise and unmatched compassion. On February 2, 2016, Providence became part of the LifePoint Health network, and shortly thereafter, changed its name to Providence Health to better represent the full complement of its services. Driven by  quality  ,  compassion  , and  Christ  , Providence treats the whole person- body, mind, and spirit. 
 Providence Health  are looking for an experienced Physical Therapy Assistant (PTA) to join our Rehab Team. 
 Essential Functions 
 
 Administers heat, cold, compression and electrical modalities to assigned patients 
 Teaches gait, transfers, proper lifting, and exercise programs under direction of the Physical Therapist 
 Instructs patients in home programs. 
 Assists Physical Therapy Aide when necessary 
 Assists with on-the-job training and supervision of Physical Therapy Aide, Volunteers and PTA students. 
 Assumes responsibilities of the Physical Therapy Aide in their absence. 
 Keeps Physical Therapist informed of any change in assigned patients 
 Monitors and reports to physical therapist when 8th visit by PT is required. 
 Demonstrates effective ability to manage patient schedule and delivery of treatment by prioritizing tasks. 
 Formulates and performs patient treatment, per plan of care, of assigned case load with focus on achieving measurable, behavioral, and functional goals with specific temporal components for Providence Downtown and Providence NE Hospitals. 
 Assists in program and policy development, case and/or research studies, data completion, and performance improvement as delegated by Director of Physical Rehabilitation Services 
 
 Job Requirements 
 Minimum Work Experience 
 Prefer one year of Physical Therapy Assistant experience 
 Minimum Education/Credentials 
 Graduate of an accredited program of Physical Therapy Assistant and hold a current valid South Carolina license to practice. 
 BLS 
 
 Required Licenses 
 [South Carolina, United States] Physical Therapy Assistants 
 South Carolina board certified
