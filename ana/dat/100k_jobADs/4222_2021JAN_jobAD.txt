 Volunteer Program Assistant 
 West Island Palliative Care Residence 
 __Kirkland, QC 
 Apply Now 
 The West Island Palliative Care Residence is the largest freestanding palliative care facility in Canada, recognized locally, nationally and internationally as a leader and center of excellence in palliative care. Over the past 17 years, the Residence has cared for over 4,500 patients and 17,000 family members. 
 We are looking for someone to join our team to fill the position of Volunteer Program Assistant. 
 Role Purpose: 
 Reporting to the Volunteer Program Manager, the Volunteer Program Assistant is responsible for managing the day-to-day logistics of the Volunteer Program in order ensure that the program remains relevant to the needs of the Residence. 
 PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITIES: 
 
 Develop, coordinate, manage and communicate the schedules for volunteers (both short-term and long-term schedules), ensuring adequate daily services 
 Send out emails and/or makes calls to find replacements for last minute cancelations 
 Keep all relevant areas (i.e. reception, kitchen, nursing, etc.) up to date with schedule changes 
 Prepare and update work tools for volunteers (i.e. “today at a glance” sheets for reception) 
 Regularly update and maintain the volunteer program database 
 Maintain volunteer master contact list 
 Manage the Volunteer Program’s email account and respond to prospective volunteers 
 Complete administrative tasks to assist in the recruitment of volunteers 
 Provide administrative support to the Volunteer Program Manager, as needed 
 Assist in the orientation and scheduling of newly hired volunteers 
 Assist in the management of logistics for volunteer-related events 
 
 General Qualifications: 
 
 The ideal candidate will have a post-secondary degree or diploma 
 Experience working with volunteers, an asset 
 Excellent knowledge of MS Office and Outlook 
 Highly organized with meticulous attention to detail 
 Commitment to accurately represent the philosophy and the goals of palliative care 
 Strong organizational, planning and effective communications skills 
 Creative and ability to think outside of the box 
 Ability to multitask, manage competing priorities and work with deadlines 
 Ability to organize workload, establish priorities and work with minimum supervision 
 Ability to act on own initiative, exercise good judgement and manage expectations 
 Adaptability to change and to learn new skills as required 
 Demonstrated ability to work as a team player 
 Professionalism, maturity, diplomacy and discretion 
 Fluently bilingual with excellent communication (written and oral) skills in both French and English 
 
 Job Type: Full-time
