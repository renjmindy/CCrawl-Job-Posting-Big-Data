 Trillium Health Partners (THP) is one of the largest community-based acute care facilities in Canada. Comprised of the Credit Valley Hospital, the Mississauga Hospital and the Queensway Health Centre, and the Reactivation Care Centre, Trillium Health Partners serves the growing and diverse populations of Mississauga, West Toronto and surrounding communities and is a teaching hospital affiliated with the University of Toronto. 
 Our Mission:  A New Kind of Health Care for a Healthier Community 
 When we set out to build our vision and future, we connected with our community - patients, families, visitors, physicians, staff and volunteers. The foundational goals of our new strategic plan - quality, access and sustainability - anchor everything we do. Our mission for a new kind of health care is built on an inter-connected system of care organized around patients - inside and outside the hospital. 
 At THP, we are relentless in providing high quality, compassionate care to our communities and take great pride in fostering an inclusive and accessible environment and we are all accountable for contributing to a healthy, safe and respectful environment for healing and promoting excellence in patient care though advancing patient and staff safety. If you are passionate about what you do, motivated to improve the health of the community, committed to excellence, quality and patient safety we would like you to join our Better Together team! 
 Job Description: 
Reporting to the Manager, Professional Practice/Clinical Resource Team this person will be responsible for the coordination of schedules and facilitation of coverage for CRT Nursing and Allied Health professionals. They will work with the Patient Care Managers/designate to collaboratively ensure coverage across the hospital (all sites) to optimize the quality of patient care offered. 
The coordinator will assist the Patient Care Managers with operational tasks associated with nursing and allied health staff (physiotherapists, occupational therapists, speech language pathologists, registered dietitians, and occupational therapy / physical therapy assistants) 
 Duties include: 
Develop schedules for CRT and prepare a deployment calendars 
Prioritize needs of departments and redeployment of nursing staff using standard hospital protocols 
Facilitate appropriate coverage of patient care units during peak times and staff shortages due to vacancies or sick calls 
Respond to either Patient Care Managers or designate to ascertain scheduling needs 
Enter staff payroll 
Build and maintain schedules and coordinate vacation requests for CRT Nurses and Allied Health professionals 
Monitor statistics and productivity for nursing and allied health professionals 
Track referrals from areas without designated coverage to determine changes in referral patterns and potential staffing requirements 
Implement and maintain a database of staff availability and contact information, and suitability 
Manage all aspects of agency utilization, booking, invoice checking/adjustments/approvals 
Maintain effective communication with all units regarding status of staffing requests 
Communicate with the Clinical Operations Managers after-hours regarding overall staffing situations 
Must be able to work in a professional, cooperative and effective manner with all fellow staff and clients 
Site of work may be Mississauga Hospital or Credit Valley Hospital 
 Qualifications: 
Post-secondary Medical Administration Diploma preferred 
Strong proficiency with MS office applications 
Proficiency with Meditech and scheduling module is preferred 
Excellent interpersonal and communication skills 
Superior organizational and time management skills 
Strong critical thinking skills to appropriate manage and prioritize scheduling demands 
Demonstrated ability to work independently under stressful conditions 
Excellent customer service skills and commitment to Trillium Health Partners values 
Satisfactory performance and attendance required 
 Internal Candidates who believe they possess the necessary qualifications and experience for this position and who have been in their current position for at least six (6) months are encouraged to apply.
