 Part Time Social Support Worker (NON-Health Care) 
 Community Residential Facility for MALE Clients 
 Male-Designated Position

 These positions are also considered on-call or filling in for full-time employees with illness or request leave. Sometimes requests for leave occur with little notice, therefore being available to respond promptly to a request to fill a shift is essential 
 Title:  Support Worker 
 Employer:  John Howard Society of Ottawa 
 Department:  Adult Justice Services 
 Reports to:  Residential Coordinator 
 Location(s):  Community Residential Facilities located within the Ottawa area 
 Job Term:  Permanent – Part Time 
 Salary:  Starting at $20.37/hour 
 Hours:  Shift work; evenings, overnights, weekend and statutory holiday available is a requirement 
 Closing Date:  Ongoing Recruitment 
 We sincerely thank all applicants who express interest in this position, though only those selected for an interview will be contacted 
 The John Howard Society of Ottawa is committed to reflecting and responding to the diversity of the people and communities we serve, and is seeking that diversity in applicants for this position 
 Agency Background: 
The John Howard Society of Ottawa is a not-for-profit, charitable organization committed to providing a broad range of evidence-based and outcome-driven community services throughout the Ottawa area. Our organization has a strong history of helping individuals, and families, who are at risk of, or have come into, conflict with the law. Our work includes the provision of prevention and intervention services, as well as advocacy, public education, and community partnerships. 
 Our diverse client group includes youth and adults, males and females, from a wide cross section of social, ethnic, linguistic and cultural backgrounds representative of our broader community. 
 Position Summary: 
Under the supervision of the Residential Coordinator, the Part Time Support Worker will perform various tasks related to building maintenance, activity monitoring, program duties and general support to the residents. 
 Qualifications: 
 Education: 
University Degree/College Diploma in the social science OR working towards completion of one 
 Language: 
Fluency in English. Fluency in French will be considered an asset as well as other languages 
 Additional Requirements: 
The Community Residential Facilities within the John Howard Society of Ottawa support male, federal offenders. Therefore, given the nature of the client group, all CRF Support Worker positions are identified as male-designated positions 
Clear Criminal Reference Check required prior to employment 
Able to pass required Government security clearance (Reliable Status) 
Regular access to a vehicle is an asset 
 Training Opportunities: 
Applied Suicide Intervention Skills Training (ASIST) 
Crisis Prevention Training – Non-Violent Crisis Intervention (NVCI) 
Understanding & Managing Aggressive Behaviour Training (UMAB) 
Mental Health First Aid Training (MHFA) 
First Aid & CPR 
 Program Level Responsibilities: 
Ensure a comprehensive understanding of CRF Operations Manual 
Understand the role/responsibilities of a Residential Coordinator, Clinical Supervisor and Case Management Team and how they interrelate 
Promote residents successfully resolving conflict after hours while maintaining client and staff safety 
To have a firm grasp in the procedural guidelines for determining when the CRF on-call worker would be required and appropriate 
Attend supervision and team meetings as necessary 
Other duties which may be identified by the Residential Coordinator or Caseworkers. This can include but is not limited to: 
Escorting residents within the community for professional appointments and basic necessities; groceries, hygiene, shopping 
Conduct Urinalysis Testing when required 
 Maintenance/Cleaning Responsibilities: 
Complete tasks in accordance with posted cleaning schedule 
Assist in keeping all common areas within the residence clean, including hallways, stairwells, laundry rooms, bathrooms and offices 
Prepare apartments for occupancy 
Cleaning after apartments have been vacated by existing residents, possible packing, labelling and storing resident’s belongings 
 Building/Activity Monitoring: 
In accordance with established guidelines, it will be necessary to record information associated with the client(s) interactions, interactions with Police Officers, Parole Supervisors and National Duty Officer or with the Support Worker’s response to various situations 
Monitor the building in accordance with the safety policy 
 Disclaimer:  This job description indicates the general nature and level of work expected of the incumbent. It is not designed to cover or contain a comprehensive listing of activities, duties or responsibilities required of the incumbent. The incumbent may be asked to perform other duties as required.
