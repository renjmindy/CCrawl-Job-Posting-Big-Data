 Job Description 
 Are you looking for a work family and a place where you can make a difference in the lives of others? Opportunity available to work in a culturally rich, diverse and rewarding healthcare environment as a Clinical Staff Pharmacist. 
 CHCW offers patient-centered care as well as an intellectually stimulating teaching environment of a Family Medicine Residency Training Program. We have an opening for a highly motivated Clinical Staff Pharmacist to join the Pharmacy Team at our Central Washington Family Medicine (CWFM) clinic. The position is  per diem  (as needed). 
 Our pharmacists are responsible for promoting patient's awareness and understanding of their medication regimen, utilizing available resources to identify opportunities to improve safety and efficacy of medication treatments, and providing supervision to ensure the accurate dispensing of medications through our on-site community pharmacies. 
 Job duties include (but are not limited to): 
 
 
 Process incoming new prescriptions, refills and transfer requests 
 
 
 Ensure accuracy, safety, and efficacy of dispensed prescriptions through thorough drug utilization review, interpretation of physician orders, and prescription verification. 
 
 
 Contribute to providing timely quality care through collaborating and assisting the team in tasks as needed, including answering phones, counting prescriptions, checking out prescriptions, and any other functions required to provide quality care and service. 
 
 
 Organize and direct pharmacy workflow to provide the highest level of quality care and service. 
 
 
 Act as the manager on duty, supervise ancillary pharmacy staff engaged in preparing, packaging, and labeling prescriptions. Organize and direct technicians' work flow; verifying their preparation and labeling of pharmaceuticals; verifying order entries, charges, and inspections. 
 
 
 Maintain safe and clean working environment by complying with procedures, rules, and regulations. 
 
 
 Initiate prior authorization requests and resolves medication billing problems with insurance providers. 
 
 
 Promote patient awareness and understanding of their medication regimen 
 
 
 Collaborate with Prescribers and clinical staff to improve health outcomes and safety 
 
 
 Serve as preceptor and mentor in the professional development of CHCW staff, pharmacy interns, medical interns, and resident providers 
 
 
 Recommends and administers vaccines 
 
 
 Operate in compliance with all governing bodies (FQHC, WA SBOP, OPA 340B, DEA, etc.) 
 
 
 Qualifications 
 
 Bachelor of Pharmacy or Doctor of Pharmacy degree from an accredited college of pharmacy  required 
 Completion of required Intern hours or equivalent to qualify for state licensure  required 
 Washington State Pharmacist License  required  (upon start date) 
 Current BLS Provider CPR Certificate 
 Certified to administer vaccines 
 Pharmacist Preceptor License preferred 
 Bilingual English/Spanish preferred ** **
