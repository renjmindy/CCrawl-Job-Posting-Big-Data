 Job 24 
 1 Why, seeing times are not hidden from the Almighty, do they that know him not see his days? 
 2 Some remove the landmarks; they violently take away flocks, and feed thereof. 
 3 They drive away the ass of the fatherless, they take the widow's ox for a pledge. 
 4 They turn the needy out of the way: the poor of the earth hide themselves together. 
 5 Behold, as wild asses in the desert, go they forth to their work; rising betimes for a prey: the wilderness yieldeth food for them and for their children. 
 6 They reap every one his corn in the field: and they gather the vintage of the wicked. 
 7 They cause the naked to lodge without clothing, that they have no covering in the cold. 
 8 They are wet with the showers of the mountains, and embrace the rock for want of a shelter. 
 9 They pluck the fatherless from the breast, and take a pledge of the poor. 
 10 They cause him to go naked without clothing, and they take away the sheaf from the hungry; 
 11 Which make oil within their walls, and tread their winepresses, and suffer thirst. 
 12 Men groan from out of the city, and the soul of the wounded crieth out: yet God layeth not folly to them. 
 13 They are of those that rebel against the light; they know not the ways thereof, nor abide in the paths thereof. 
 14 The murderer rising with the light killeth the poor and needy, and in the night is as a thief. 
 15 The eye also of the adulterer waiteth for the twilight, saying, No eye shall see me: and disguiseth his face. 
 16 In the dark they dig through houses, which they had marked for themselves in the daytime: they know not the light. 
 17 For the morning is to them even as the shadow of death: if one know them, they are in the terrors of the shadow of death. 
 18 He is swift as the waters; their portion is cursed in the earth: he beholdeth not the way of the vineyards. 
 19 Drought and heat consume the snow waters: so doth the grave those which have sinned. 
 20 The womb shall forget him; the worm shall feed sweetly on him; he shall be no more remembered; and wickedness shall be broken as a tree. 
 21 He evil entreateth the barren that beareth not: and doeth not good to the widow. 
 22 He draweth also the mighty with his power: he riseth up, and no man is sure of life. 
 23 Though it be given him to be in safety, whereon he resteth; yet his eyes are upon their ways. 
 24 They are exalted for a little while, but are gone and brought low; they are taken out of the way as all other, and cut off as the tops of the ears of corn. 
 25 And if it be not so now, who will make me a liar, and make my speech nothing worth?

 The real question Job is asking is one that is still asked by many today: WHY DOESN'T GOD DO THINGS THE WAY I THINK HE SHOULD? 
 People ask this all the time with slight variations: If there is a God, then where was He when that man killed innocent people in a church, when terrorists murder innocent children for their faith or behead captives who refuse to convert? If God is good, then why are children abused or abducted and sold as sex-slaves? Why do tsunamis and earthquakes and tornados and hurricanes take homes and lives and wipe out whole communities in an instant? Why doesn't God intervene in injustices? Why doesn't He grant my every request and make it easy for me to believe? Why doesn't He show Himself? Speak audibly to me? 
 It seems men are always trying to make God in our own image. Make Him think like us. Act like us. Do what we expect Him to do, or all that we ask Him to do. 
 The carnal mind wants a faith that, quiet honestly, requires no faith. We would rather have a Santa Claus that we can control and manipulate with our actions than a sovereign God who expects us to accept His will even when we don't understand it. 
 Paul tells Timothy to "fight the good fight of faith, lay hold on eternal life," (1 Tim. 6:12). Later, he says, "I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course, I have kept the faith," (2 Timothy 4:7).

It's standing faithful when God says "no," or when He doesn't seem to say anything at all. It rises up in the face of doubt and fear and all the world's "proof" that there is no God and proclaims His name anyway. It doesn't waiver when disappointed. It trusts even in the midst of a crumbling world. 
 Those with this true saving faith understand that they may never understand the ways of God. Calvary is all the proof they need that God is for them and that He is working for their eternal good. They stop saying things like "God, if You love me, You'll do this or that." They stop questioning God when things don't go the way they think they should. They seek God's will rather than their own, and they surrender themselves completely to the sovereignty of the One they cannot see but Whom they have no doubt has called them by name.

 24 They are exalted for a little while, but are gone and brought low; they are taken out of the way as all other, and cut off as the tops of the ears of corn. 
 Ever notice the tip or narrow end of an ear of corn, how the kernels are not as big and juicy as the rest of the ear? This is because the kernels at the top of the ear the last to be fertilized and have less time to mature. For those in the corn business, these immature kernels cost more to process and yield less product than the is worth the time. Thus, cutting off the tops of the ears is a process to remove the least fruitful portion of the harvest.
