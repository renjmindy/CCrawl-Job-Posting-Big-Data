 Administrative Assistant I 
 Strathcona County 
 __Sherwood Park, AB 
 Apply Now 
 Job Description 
 As the Administrative Assistant I you will provide senior level administrative support to the Manager of Human Services & Innovation to ensure a structured and organized operation that meets the goals, objectives and commitments of the branch, creating and maintaining professional rapport with all internal and external contacts. 
 Responsibilities 
 Act as the first point of contact for the Manager’s office; use discretion and judgement regarding the flow of information 
Maintain calendars, arrange meetings, prepare meeting packages, track action items, research and support preparation of reports, presentations, documents and correspondence 
Manage electronic and hard copy corporate records, create or revises forms, coordinate FOIP requests, tracking of job requirements (i.e. first aid, criminal record checks, suicide prevention expiry dates) 
Coordinate registrations for professional development 
Being responsible for creating processes and procedures by analyzing current practices and projects and looking for efficiencies 
Provide administrative support to the Director as backup for the Administrative Assistant II when needed 
Actively participate as a member of the department’s administrative team 
 Skills and Abilities 
 Knowledge of administrative principles and practices 
Excellent interpersonal, customer service and communication skills (verbal and written) 
Excellent time management skills, ability to manage multiple tasks all with minimal supervision 
Ability to handle confidential information with a high degree of judgement and discretion 
Ability to work independently, with flexibility and adaptability 
Proficiency with Microsoft Office 365 and Adobe 
Ability to set and enforce clear boundaries 
 Qualifications 
 High School diploma with five years of diversified office administration experience 
Diploma or certificate in office administration is an asset 
Municipal government experience in a related role is preferred; experience working in a Social Services setting would be considered an asset 
Experience working with Microsoft office suite is required 
 Conditions of Employment 
 Submission of Satisfactory Vulnerable Sector Check and Child Intervention Record Check 
 Equivalencies of education and experience may be considered. In addition to providing a dynamic working environment, we offer a competitive salary and benefits package. 
 Shortlisted candidates will be required to complete a pre-employment assessment at the Strathcona County Human Resources office as well as provide proof of educational and professional credentials as noted in the resume. 
 This competition may be used to fill future vacancies for similar positions within the next 6 months. 
 Competition #300-2019-EXT-FCS closes November 10, 2019 
 Thank you for your interest in Strathcona County. We only contact candidates selected for interviews. 
 A cover letter must be submitted along with a resume describing the interest in this position.
