 Engineering Specialist, Aircraft Loads-MON17407 
 Description 
 BOMBARDIER 
 At Bombardier, our employees work together to evolve mobility worldwide - one good idea at a time. If you have a good idea, we’ll provide the environment where it will thrive and grow into a great product or customer experience. Your ideas are our fuel. 
 In your role, you will: 
- 
Perform aeroelastic and aeroservoelastic simulations in support of new aircraft design studies and continued airworthiness of in-service aircraft 
 - 
Develop dynamic FEM models to compute airframe design loads and component design loads (high lift and control surface) 
 - 
Use engineering judgment on selecting the appropriate methods, techniques and tools in resolving complex issues 
 - 
Perform dynamic aeroelastic response analyses for tuned discrete gust and continuous turbulence with and without the effects from fly-by-wire 
 - 
Support and interact with other engineering disciplines such as Flight Characteristics, Structures, Mass Properties, Systems, Fly-By-Wire, Landing Gear and Flight Test 
 - 
Define restrictions and special instructions for flight test procedures based on loads analyses 
 - 
Improve and document loads analyses methods 
 - 
Provide Loads support for both flight test and in-service AOGs 
 - 
Document analyses and findings in engineering certification reports 
 Qualifications 
 As our ideal candidate, 
 
 You have a Bachelor's or Master’s degree in Aerospace or Mechanical Engineering. A higher education would be an asset 
 
 - 
You have at least 10 years of experience in external loads analyses in the aerospace industry 
 - 
You have a good understanding of the FAR 25 certification requirements 
 - 
You have a strong background in static and dynamic aeroelastic loads analyses 
 - 
You can develop FEM models, compute airframe design loads and component design loads (high lift and control surface) 
 - 
You have previous experience using MSC Nastran for Dynamic/Aeroelastic analysis; DMAP is an asset 
 - 
You have a good understanding of FEM, unsteady aerodynamics, and frequency-based data analysis 
 - 
You are familiar with concepts of linearizing controls laws to be used for TDG and PSD gust analyses 
 - 
You have a good knowledge of engine fan blade out and windmilling dynamic loads analyses 
 
 You have experience in working in flight test environment, collecting flight test data and comparing flight test data with analyses data 
 You have a good understanding of the instrumentation (strain gauge, accelerometers, pressure sensors) needed for flight test measurements 
 You have advanced programming knowledge in Matlab, Python, Fortran, C/C++, and Linux/Windows scripting 
 You have good organizational skills in planning work activities and complete tasks within budget and on schedule 
 You have good analytical thinking, problem solving, and communication skills (both written and verbal) and ability to work under pressure
