 General Manager - Northeast Region 
 Miller Paving Limited 
 __New Liskeard, ON 
 Apply Now 
 Lead the development and implementation of a quality and safety focused based business culture. This includes enhancing or introducing new quality, team, performance, employee relations and OH&S management approaches and standards 
Based on an evaluation of existing resources and market trends, identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats within the market area and develop a business plan that strategically positions the business in a favorable position 
Improve margins through the right combination of product, services, strategic placement and cost reduction initiatives. Drive for continuous business performance improvement 
Ensure optimal utilization of assets, including but not limited to equipment, plants and aggregate sites 
Oversee staff and functions ensuring implementation of Miller and industry best practices designed to provide the best quality product to our customers 
Address customer needs by developing and implementing timely business solutions 
Represent Miller within the respective market in industry and community organizations 
Oversee all activities related to estimating, planning, scheduling, equipment and labour requirements and material determination including ensuring accurate bids for all submitted tenders 
Oversee operations of the project team and maintain relationships with sub-trades and clients 
Responsible for subcontract negotiations, liaison with owners, engineers, and sub-trades, management of technical and financial aspects of contracts 
Communicate company initiatives and pursues best practices and develop a high performance team with the goal to deliver positive margins 
 Requirements: 
Creative but pragmatic entrepreneurial approach, with record of accomplishment and experience of 10+ years in leading business growth and change in infrastructure construction and maintenance 
A civil engineering degree or diploma. Professional designation like P.Eng or CET will be considered an asset 
Results orientation, a strong work ethic and a team focused “hands on” leadership approach 
Experience and ability to understand operations from plans through to front line issues and be available and connected to senior management and staff 
Proven experience working with both union and non union employee groups 
Ability to build strong respectful relationships and communicate effectively and persuasively with different stakeholders 
Comprehensive knowledge of budgeting, financial management practices and accounting principles 
An ability to prioritize work and manage multiple complex priorities with a sense of urgency 
Valid Class G Driver’s license and flexibility to meet the significant travel requirements of the role (up to 50%) 
 Unsolicited resumes from staffing firms will not be accepted. We will contact candidates selected for an interview. Accommodations are available upon request.
