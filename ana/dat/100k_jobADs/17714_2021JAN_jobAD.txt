 Recruiting is a key component to the success of Fleet Feet. Our Talent Acquistion Manager is accountable for driving recruiting strategies and identifying internal and external candidates across our company-owned locations. This person will lead the sourcing efforts for specific roles, collaborate with leaders to provide top-notch candidate and onboarding experiences, and drive hiring initiatives that shape our employer branding strategy. A significant focus will also be on diversity, equity, and inclusion. This person will assist with strategizing and engaging with diverse partnerships and activities that will build brand awareness, conduct market research to understand where diverse talent pools exist, and explore initiatives to tap into those talent pools. This position will have the opportunity to create, implement, and launch new processes, procedures, and policies while supporting a fast-paced retail leader in the running industry. 
 Essential Duties & Responsibilities 
 
 Partner with Human Resources, Operations, and other company-owned leaders on business workforce planning and staffing needs for our company-owned locations 
 Coach and train hiring managers on the recruitment life cycle and best practice from objectively screening candidates to making an offer 
 Create a fully-paid internship program focused on BIPOC recruitment that overcomes perceived barriers of participation related to location, transportation, or awareness. 
 Partner with, educate and consult with hiring managers on recruiting process, strategy, and expectations, ensuring a smooth process for candidates and new hires through the recruiting process 
 Develop and maintain a network of professional contacts to help identify, source, and engage industry leaders in building a qualified talent pipeline 
 Utilize proactive, creative, and cost-effective recruitment methods to attract and source candidates including leveraging online recruiting resources, in-house Applicant Tracking System (ATS), and driving internal employee referrals 
 Leverage internal resources to build comprehensive hiring strategies around Employment Branding, Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion, and College Recruiting 
 Ensure that the hiring process and hiring managers comply with Fleet Feet policies and procedures and with employment laws and regulations 
 Review resumes and credentials for appropriate skills, experience, and knowledge based on position requirements 
 Prepare candidates and set expectations appropriately for interviews by providing detailed information on the company, business unit, or retail location they’re interviewing with and job description details 
 Where appropriate, perform detailed reference checking on selected candidates and review results with hiring managers 
 Maintain records on company-wide recruiting activities. Utilize an automated applicant tracking system in compliance with staffing requirements to support the recruitment process 
 Proactively seek and research opportunities to add value to all Fleet Feet entities through effective policies, best practice, and technology 
 Represent the company in community service activities 
 Update job knowledge by participating in educational opportunities; reading professional publications; maintaining personal networks; participating in professional organizations to stay abreast of employment laws and industry trends 
 Other duties as required and assigned by Human Resources. These may, on occasion, be unrelated to the position described here 
 
 Qualifications 
 
 Bachelor’s Degree or equivalent work experience 
 3+ years of full life-cycle recruitment within a Corporate, University or Retail setting specifically focused on diversity, equity, and inclusion efforts 
 An understanding of the opportunities and challenges in building a diverse talent pool/pipeline and the tenacity to overcome obstacles and challenges 
 Experience developing and partnering with professional organizations and networks spezializing in Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion 
 Excellent organizational, interpersonal, and communications skills 
 Strong attention to detail 
 Ability to partner with and influence Hiring Managers as a talent advisor 
 Highly competent with candidate sourcing tools, applicant tracking systems, and the ability to quickly learn and adopt new technology as required 
 Proven ability to source, engage, and close passive candidates on niche roles using tools including LinkedIn, ATS, etc. Recruiting on a variety of different types of roles, managing a high volume of open roles and competing priorities 
 Ability to deliver high-energy presentations internally and externally to audiences 
 Knowledge of recruiting metrics, assessment tools and techniques, and the ability to use data to tell a story about results and impact 
 Think strategically about building candidate pipelines through engagement, sourcing, and programs 
 Experience in high growth, fast-paced environment 
 Experience working with current best practice and familiarity with industry trends in functional areas 
 Successful track record of building and maintaining relationships with candidates, peers, industry members, university partners, and leaders 
 Established path of success in candidate identification, attraction, and development 
 Strong talent assessment skills 
 Travel 5-10% of time (when business travel resumes) 
 
 Other Requirements: 
 
 Position requires occasional walking and standing with light (10-15lbs.) carrying, lifting, pushing, or pulling. The position is a sedentary position and requires the employee to have close visual acuity to perform job requirements. The position requires frequent talking, hearing, and repetitive motion with 
the hands, wrists, and/or fingers. This position is located within a distribution center environment and is not substantially exposed to adverse environmental conditions.
