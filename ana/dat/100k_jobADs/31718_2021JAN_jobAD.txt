 Operational Clinical Lead - Edinburgh 
 Operational Clinical Lead 
 Edinburgh 
 Our client, a leading provider of life science solutions, are now looking for an Operational Scientific Lead to manage their team on site in Edinburgh with their work with Covid-19 testing and clinical trials for a fixed term contract of 12 months that could well be extended further on a rolling basis. 
 The Benefits 
 - Competitive Salary 
 - Excellent benefits 
 This is an exceptional opportunity to help impact the pandemic and play a part of the resolution of this global crisis. 
 The Role 
 On-site Lead(s) with experience in managing or supervising teams of laboratory technicians and support staff within a scientific, infectious sciences or virology laboratory.  The post holder will lead the operational delivery team for Covid-19 testing services to support the UK response to the pandemic. 
 Provide and operate highly specialized, containerized COVID-19 testing laboratories to support increased testing demand.  In this context they are intended for use at locations to be determined by NHS in accordance with their demands. 
 The laboratories were first deployed by our Client in Luxembourg to provide optimised, highly automated workflows, from COVID-19 adapted swab collection tubes to pooled RT_qPCR read-outs that maintain (or surpass) individual test sensitivities. 
 High levels of throughput with uncompromising quality are ideally supported by purpose-designed laboratories where one single test type is being performed and where all components are tuned to this singular test. 
 Testing methodologies include RT_qPCR in a highly automated workflow involving several different high throughput liquid handling robots, as well as digital tracking software. 
 In addition, testing methods might change dynamically and drastically and the postholder will be required to adapt and  contribute to the evolution of test regimes. 
 Operationally the role works to a clinical rota requiring leadership of clinical technicians, responsibility for SOPs, regulatory compliance, participation in clinical audits and will contribute to the training of scientific and technical staff. 
 Person Requirements: 
 
 
 3-5 Years Experieince in managing or supervising teams within a virology or microbiology clinical lab environment 
 
 
 Clinical Scientific Registration with he HCPC is desirable although not a necessity 
 
 
 Experience in Quality Assurance and Audit Procedures 
 
 
 In depth knowledge of molecular methods in clinical virology and real time PCR Methods 
 
 
 Experience of automated robotic laboratory sampling processes, training and technical support will be provided 
 
 
 Detailed understanding and experieince in supporting LIMS , training and support will be provided on specific systems 
 
 
 Experience in automated robotics lab sampling processes, training and technical support will be provided
