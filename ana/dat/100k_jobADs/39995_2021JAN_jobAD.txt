 How to Quickly Produce a Job Board in WordPress (NO HTML Needed) 
 __ April 23, 2019 
 Do you wish to produce a task board in WordPress? Job boards permit people to find jobs and companies to post their own task listings. 
 With the growing trend of the gig economy, beginning a job board has become a popular   online company concept  You can generate income by accepting payments for job listings. 
 In this post, we will show you how to quickly produce a task board in WordPress and make cash from it. You can likewise use this tutorial to include a job board to an existing WordPress website. 
 Starting with Your Task Board Website in WordPress 
 You will require the following products to produce a job board site with WordPress. 
 
 A domain (This will be your website's address. Example, wpbeginner.com) 
 A webhosting account (This is where all your site files will be stored) 
 A job board extension for WordPress 
 Paid job listings add-on (if you desire to accept payments for job listings)

 Now that you have WordPress ready, it is time to develop a task board site with WordPress. 
 Setting up a Job Board in WordPress 
 The first thing you require to do is set up and trigger the   WP Job Supervisor  plugin. For more information, see our step by step guide on   how to install a WordPress plugin 
 Upon activation, you will need to develop a few pages that the plugin will utilize to show various areas of your task board. 
 On each page, you just require to get in a title and after that add a shortcode. 
 Let's begin. The first page we're going to create will be for jobs. Simply produce a brand-new page and enter Jobs in the title field. In the content area, you need to add a shortcode block and paste the following shortcode inside it: 
 [jobs] 
 After adding the shortcode, click the publish button to save your modifications. 
 Next, you will need to duplicate the procedure to produce a 'Submit Task Noting' page and include the following shortcode: 
 [submit_job_form] 
 Lastly, you require to create a 'Jobs Dashboard' page and go into the following shortcode in the content location: 
 [job_dashboard] 
 Now that you have created all the required pages, let's proceed to plugin's settings. 
 Head over to  Job Listings " Settings  page and click the 'Pages' tab. 
 From here, you require to pick the pages you have created earlier and then click the save changes button to keep your settings. 
 After that, you need to examine other settings on the page. Let's start with the 'General' tab initially. 
 On the basic page, you can choose the date format, add Google Maps API key, or delete data when you uninstall the plugin. 
 Note:  Google Maps API key needs you to offer billing info, and you will be charged based on your usage. If you do not wish to use Google Maps API, then you can leave this choice unattended. 
 After that, you can switch to the 'Task Listings' tab. There are a variety of options on this page to manage the screen and functions available for task listings. You need to review and adjust them to your own requirements. 
 Likewise, you can manage the task listings submissions by changing to the 'Task Submission' tab. From here, you can control new account settings, submission moderation, modify controls, and more. 
 To safeguard your site against automated spam, you require to change to the reCaptcha tab and enable it by adding the API secrets. You will find a link to create the required API secrets next to each choice. 
 WP Job Supervisor also sends out alerts to site administrators and users. You can manage them under the 'Em ail Notifications' tab. 
 Bonus offer tip: we recommend utilizing the   WP Mail SMTP  plugin to repair the common   WordPress not sending out email issue 
 When you are done, don't forget to click on the save modifications button to save your settings. 
 Including New Job Listings 
 Depending upon your settings, logged-in users can submit tasks straight from Submit Jobs page you developed previously. 
 As an   administrator , you can directly include jobs from the WordPress admin location. 
 Just go to the  Job Listings " Add New  page and supply a title, description, and the job type for the job listing you are adding. 
 After getting in the title, description and selecting a job type, you require to scroll down to Task Listing Data section. There you can offer more details about the task such as place, application email or URL, business information, business logo design, job listing expiration date, etc. 
 After filling the job listing data, you can preview the task by clicking the preview button. 
 After that you either add more details if you need to or just click on Publish button to post the listing. 
 To handle existing job listings, you need to go to  Job Listings " All Task Listings  page in the WordPress admin area. 
 There you will be able to see all the task listing published on your task board. You can click the pencil icon to edit a job listing, see the filled tasks, and erase existing task listings. 
 Your website visitors will be able to view all task listings by visiting the jobs page you created earlier. 
 Visited users will also be able to manage their task listings by checking out the tasks control panel page you developed previously. 
 Adding Paid Task Listings 
 You may have seen several trusted websites including a job board to produce additional earnings from their site. If your blog site has a good track record amongst market peers, then companies wouldn't mind paying a little fee to get their job posting noted on your site. 
 To allow this feature, you will initially require to install and trigger the   Simple Paid Listings  plugin. For more information, see our action by step guide on   how to set up a WordPress plugin 
 It is a paid add-on for WP Job Manager and enables you to accept payments for task listings using PayPal or Stripe. 
 Upon activation, you require to go to  Job Listings " Settings  page and switch to the 'Paid Listings' tab. 
 On this tab, you can select your favored payment gateway and offer API information. 
 Note:  You will need to make it possible for SSL/ HTTPS on your site to get payments. See our post on   how to get complimentary SSL certificate for your site  for in-depth guidelines.
